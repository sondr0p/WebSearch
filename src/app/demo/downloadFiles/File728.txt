           <h1>Federal Register Publications</h1> 
           <div id="shared"> 
            <p>The Administrative Committee of the Federal Register (ACFR) oversees the functions of the Federal Register publications system. &nbsp; <a class="moreLink" href="/federal-register/acfr/">Learn More</a></p> 
           </div> 
           <!--

           <h2><a name="updates"></a>Stay Updated</h2> 
           <blockquote>
            Sign up for Electronic Mailing Lists to receive up-to-date information by e-mail.
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Daily Federal Register</h3> 
           <p><a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmzp" target="_blank">Today's Issue</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;<a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmz7" target="_blank">Subscriptions and RSS Feeds</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;<a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmzA" target="_blank">Search Online</a></p> 
           <blockquote>
            The official daily publication for rules, proposed rules, and notices of Federal agencies and organizations, as well as executive orders and other Presidential documents.&nbsp;
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmSU" target="_blank">Learn More</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Code of Federal Regulations (CFR)</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            The codification of the general and permanent rules published in the Federal Register by the executive departments and agencies of the Federal Government. It is divided into 50 titles that represent broad areas subject to Federal regulation. Each volume of the CFR is updated once each calendar year and is issued on a quarterly basis.&nbsp;
            <a _blank="" href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmSP">Learn More</a>&nbsp;| 
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmSQ" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Electronic Code of Federal Regulations (e-CFR)</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            The Electronic Code of Federal Regulations (e-CFR) is a currently updated version of the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR). The e-CFR is an editorial compilation of CFR material and Federal Register amendments, not an official legal edition of the CFR. The OFR updates the material in the e-CFR on a daily basis. The current update status appears at the top of all e-CFR web pages.&nbsp;
            <a href="/federal-register/cfr/about.html" target="_blank">Learn More</a>&nbsp;| 
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmSV" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Compilation of Presidential Documents</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            The official publication of presidential statements, messages, remarks, and other materials released by the White House Press Secretary. 
            <a href="/federal-register/publications/presidential-compilation.html" target="_blank">Learn More</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmsq" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Federal Register Index</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            This index provides descriptive entries and Federal Register page numbers for documents published in the daily Federal Register. It includes entries, with select metadata for all documents published in a&nbsp;calendar year. 
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmt6" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>List of CFR Sections Affected (LSA)</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            Lists proposed, new, and amended Federal regulations that have been published in the Federal Register since the most recent revision date of a CFR title. Each LSA issue is cumulative and contains the CFR part and section numbers, a description of its status, and the Federal Register page number where the change(s) may be found.&nbsp;
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmuT" target="_blank">Learn More</a>&nbsp;| 
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmAb" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Privacy Act Issuances</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            Contains descriptions of Federal agency systems of records maintained on individuals and procedures Federal agencies follow to assist individuals who request information about their records. The categories of documents in this database are: individual Privacy Act system descriptions, computer matching program notices, and agency record keeping policies and practices.&nbsp;
            <a href="/federal-register/publications/privacy-act.html" target="_blank">Learn More</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmSG" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Public &amp; Private Laws</h3> 
           <p><a href="/federal-register/laws/updates.html" target="_blank">Recent Public Laws</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;<a href="/federal-register/laws/updates.html" target="_blank">Receive E-mail Updates</a></p> 
           <blockquote>
            Contains laws signed by the President. The database for the current session of Congress is updated when the publication of a slip law is authorized by OFR. Laws signed by the President become known as "slip laws." At the end of every session of Congress, "slip laws" are compiled in to the Statutes at Large and are know as "session laws." Every six years, the "session laws" are incorporated into the United States Code, the codification of all general and permanent laws of the United States.&nbsp;
            <a href="/federal-register/laws/index.html" target="_blank">Learn More</a>&nbsp;| 
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmA8" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Public Papers of the Presidents</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            Official series of Presidential writings, addresses, and remarks of a public nature. Volumes covering the administrations of Presidents Hoover, Truman, Eisenhower, Kennedy, Johnson, Nixon, Ford, Carter, Reagan, Bush, and Clinton are included in the Public Papers series, though they are not all available online at this time. 
            <a href="/files/federal-register/publications/presidential-papers.html" target="_blank">Learn More</a>&nbsp;| 
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmAz" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>United States Government Manual</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            Provides comprehensive information on the agencies of the legislative, judicial, and executive branches. It also includes information on quasi-official agencies, international organizations in which the United States participates, and boards, commissions, and committees. 
            <a href="/federal-register/publications/government-manual.html">Learn More</a>&nbsp;| 
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmAS" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>United States Statutes at Large</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            The United States Statutes at Large, typically referred to as the Statutes at Large, is the permanent collection of all laws and resolutions enacted during each session of Congress. The Statutes at Large is prepared and published by the Office of the Federal Register (OFR), National Archives and Records Administration (NARA). GPO Access contains the statutes enacted in the 108th Congress, first session, Volume 117, with future volumes to be added as they become available.&nbsp;
            <a href="/federal-register/publications/laws.html#statutes" target="_blank">Learn More</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;
            <a href="https://go.usa.gov/xNmsc" target="_blank">Search Online</a>
           </blockquote> 
           <h3>Document Drafting Handbook.&nbsp;<a href="/federal-register/write/handbook/" target="_blank">Read Online</a></h3> 
           <h3>Unified Agenda</h3> 
           <blockquote>
            Summarizes the rules and proposed rules that each Federal agency expects to issue during the next six months. The Unified Agenda is published twice a year (usually in April and October) in the Federal Register (FR).
            <br>
