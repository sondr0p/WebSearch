       <h2 id="item-213705">Immediate Food Assistance</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>If you’re hungry now:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Call the National Hunger Hotline at 1-866-3-HUNGRY (<a href="tel:18663486479">1-866-348-6479</a>) or 1-877-8-HAMBRE (<a href="tel:18778426273">1-877-842-6273</a>) in Spanish. The hotline operates Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM to 10:00 PM Eastern Time.</p></li>
       <li><p><a href="https://healthfinder.gov/FindServices/SearchContext.aspx?topic=322&amp;service=148">Find a local food bank</a>.</p></li>
       <li><p>Contact community or religious organizations.</p></li>
      </ul>

      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-35787">Food Stamps (SNAP Food Benefits)</h2> 
      </header> 
      <h3>What help is available through food stamps (SNAP food benefits)?</h3>
      <p>If you have a low income, the <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/supplemental-nutrition-assistance-program-snap">Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)</a>, formerly known as "food stamps," is a federal nutrition program that can help you stretch your food budget.</p>
      <h3>Am I eligible for SNAP?</h3>
      <p>To determine if you are eligible, you must meet certain <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/eligibility">requirements including resource and income limits</a>.&nbsp;</p>
      <h3>How do I apply for SNAP?</h3>
      <p>Find the <a href="http://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/apply">online application for your state</a>, local office addresses, and phone numbers. You may &nbsp;also apply in person at a state or local office.</p>
      <h3>Is there anything else I need to know about SNAP?</h3>
      <ul>
       <li><p>You get benefits each month on a plastic electronic benefits transfer (EBT) card, which you use like a credit or debit card to purchase eligible food items at your local authorized SNAP grocery stores&nbsp;and some farmers markets.</p></li>
       <li><p>Some states have <a href="http://www.fns.usda.gov/ebt/state-ebt-websites">websites set up for managing your benefits</a>. If your state doesn't have an EBT management website, you can contact your <a href="http://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/state-informationhotline-numbers">state SNAP office</a> to check your benefits.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>How do I file a complaint about SNAP?</h3>
      <p>If you need to file a complaint concerning your food stamps or attempt to get food stamps, the following resources can help:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>If you have <a href="http://asktheexpert.custhelp.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/1595">questions about your benefit amount</a>, contact your <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/state-informationhotline-numbers">local field office</a> to request a hearing.</p></li>
       <li><p>File a <a href="https://www.usda.gov/oig/hotline.htm">complaint online or by phone about a SNAP retailer</a>. You will need to give the name and the location of the store. You may remain anonymous if you choose.</p></li>
       <li><p>If you suspect fraud or abuse of the SNAP program, contact your <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/integrity/fraud-reporting-by-state">state's SNAP fraud hotline or website</a>.</p></li>
       <li><p><a href="https://www.ascr.usda.gov/node/119">Learn how to file a complaint</a> if you believe you have experienced discrimination in the SNAP program because of age, sex, color, race, disability, religious creed, national origin, limited English proficiency, or political beliefs.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>SNAP Information For Retailers</h3>
      <p>Learn how to work with SNAP if you <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/retailer">are a retailer</a> or if you <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/farmer-producer">operate a farmers market</a>.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-213688"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-213688">Learn About the WIC Program for Women and Infants</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>This short-term program can help you get healthy food for yourself and your young children.</p>
      <h3>What Help is Available Through the WIC Program?</h3>
      <p>Many low-income women and young children can get healthy food to add to their diet. It’s available through the <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/about-wic-wic-glance">Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants and Children (WIC)</a>. WIC also offers nutrition counseling and referrals to health, welfare, and social services.</p>
      <h3>Am I Eligible for the WIC Program?</h3>
      <ul>
       <li><p>If you’re applying for yourself, you must be at least one of the following:</p>
        <ul>
         <li><p>Pregnant</p></li>
         <li><p>Breastfeeding</p></li>
         <li><p>Within six months of having given&nbsp;birth or pregnancy ending</p></li>
        </ul></li>
       <li><p>If you’re applying for your children, they must be under five years old.</p></li>
       <li><p>You must meet other <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/wic-eligibility-requirements">WIC eligibility requirements</a> based on your income, your health, and where you live.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>How Do I Apply for WIC?</h3>
      <p>Contact <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/contacts?f%5B0%5D=program%3A32">your state or local WIC agency</a> for an appointment. When you call, someone will tell you where to apply and what to bring with you.</p>
      <h3>Who Do I Contact for Extra Help with WIC?</h3>
      <p>For more information, <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/contacts?f%5B0%5D=program%3A32">contact your state or local WIC agency</a>. You can also call <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/toll-free-numbers-wic-state-agencies">its toll-free number</a>.</p>
      <h3>Is There Anything Else I Need to Know About WIC?</h3>
      <p>If your agency doesn’t have enough money to serve everyone who needs WIC, it will <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/wic-eligibility-priority-system">maintain a waiting list and use a priority system</a> to decide who will get WIC benefits first.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-213690"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-213690">Learn About Free Food Programs for School-Age Children</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>These programs can help you get healthy food for your children at their school, childcare center, or after-school program.</p>
      <h3>What Food Help is Available for School-Age Kids?</h3>
      <p>Kids from qualified, low-income households can get healthy meals or milk at their school or childcare center.</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>The <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/nslp/national-school-lunch-program-nslp">National School Lunch Program</a> and <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/sbp/school-breakfast-program-sbp">School Breakfast Program </a>provide free or reduced-price meals on school days.</p></li>
       <li><p>The <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/smp/special-milk-program">Special Milk Program</a> provides milk to kids at schools and childcare centers that don’t offer the breakfast and lunch programs.</p></li>
       <li><p>The <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/sfsp/summer-food-service-program">Summer Food Service Program</a> offers free, healthy breakfasts and lunches to kids over summer break.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Are My Kids Eligible for Food Programs Through School?</h3>
      <p>Your kids automatically qualify for free meals or milk if:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>You receive&nbsp;<a href="https://www.usa.gov/benefits#item-35787">SNAP (food stamps)</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.usa.gov/benefits#item-36602">TANF (welfare)</a>, or the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/fdpir/food-distribution-program-indian-reservations-fdpir">Food Distribution Program on Indian Reservations</a> (FDPIR)</p></li>
       <li><p>They are <a href="https://www.childwelfare.gov/topics/outofhome/foster-care/">foster children</a> under the legal responsibility of a foster care agency or court</p></li>
       <li><p>They’re enrolled in <a href="https://eclkc.ohs.acf.hhs.gov/">Head Start</a></p></li>
      </ul>
      <p>Your kids may qualify for meals or milk if your household income is within the <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/school-meals/income-eligibility-guidelines">Federal Income Eligibility Guidelines</a>. These guidelines are based on <a href="https://aspe.hhs.gov/poverty-guidelines">federal poverty guidelines</a>.</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>If your household income is no more than 130 percent of the poverty level, they should qualify for free meals.</p></li>
       <li><p>If your income is no more than 185 percent of the poverty level, they should qualify for reduced-price meals.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <p>The summer food service program is open to all kids and teens 18 and under at locations around the country. Find a <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/summerfoodrocks">site near you.</a></p>
      <h3>How Do I Apply for Food Help for School-Age Children?</h3>
      <p>At the beginning of the school year or at any time if circumstances change, <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/school-meals/applying-free-and-reduced-price-school-meals">submit an application from the school</a>.</p>
      <h3>How Do I Complain/Who Do I Contact for Extra Help?</h3>
      <p>Contact your local school or school district for more information.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-213706"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-213706">Learn About Free Food Programs for Seniors</h2> 
      </header> 
      <h3>What Help is Available?</h3>
      <p>Most states offer these food programs for low-income seniors:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p><a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/sfmnp/senior-farmers-market-nutrition-program-sfmnp">Senior Farmers’ Market Nutrition Program</a>, which provides coupons for fresh fruits, vegetables, honey, and herbs at farmers’ markets, roadside stands, and community farms.</p></li>
       <li><p><a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/csfp/commodity-supplemental-food-program-csfp">Commodity Supplemental Food Program</a>, which provides a monthly package of <a href="https://fns-prod.azureedge.net/sites/default/files/csfp/CSFP-slick-sheet.pdf">nutritious foods</a>.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Am I Eligible?</h3>
      <p>Low-income seniors (age 60 and older) who live in an area that offers either of these programs may apply for the program(s) offered.</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Check the <a href="https://fns-prod.azureedge.net/sites/default/files/wic/S-FMNP-CVV_Map.pdf">farmers’ market program map</a> to see if your state offers this benefit. Find your <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/sfmnp/sfmnp-income-guidelines">maximum household income</a> for participation.</p></li>
       <li><p>Check the <a href="https://fns-prod.azureedge.net/sites/default/files/csfp/programFactSheet-csfp.pdf">supplemental food program fact sheet</a> to see if the program is authorized in your state. Find your <a href="https://fns-prod.azureedge.net/sites/default/files/csfp/income-guidelines.pdf">household income limit</a> for participation. &nbsp;</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>How Do I Apply?</h3>
      <ul>
       <li><p>For the farmers’ market program, <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/contacts?f%5B0%5D=program%3A30">contact your state program office</a>. If you get <a href="https://www.usa.gov/benefits#item-35787">SNAP (food stamps)</a>&nbsp;or Commodity Supplemental Food packages, contact that program’s local office.</p></li>
       <li><p>For the Commodity Supplemental Food Program, call <a href="tel:18663486479">1-866-348-6479</a> to find your state contact or find the <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/contacts?f%5B0%5D=program%3A26">commodity food distribution contact</a> closest to you.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Is There Anything Else I Need to Know?</h3>
      <p>You may qualify for other food assistance programs including <a href="https://www.usa.gov/benefits#item-35787">SNAP (food stamps)</a>&nbsp;and the <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/fdpir/food-distribution-program-indian-reservations-fdpir">Food Distribution Program on Indian Reservations</a>. Check the <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/program/assistance-people-all-ages">program websites</a> or contact your local senior community center to learn more.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-214136"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-214136">Get Emergency Help Paying for Food</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>You may be able to get short-term financial help for your family’s food following a disaster. If the president authorizes <a href="https://www.disasterassistance.gov/">individual disaster assistance</a> for your area, you may qualify for <a href="https://fns-prod.azureedge.net/sites/default/files//snap/D-SNAP_Disaster.pdf">D-SNAP</a>. D-SNAP is the disaster supplemental nutrition assistance program.&nbsp;D-SNAP provides one month’s worth of benefits on a debit-type card that you can use at most grocery stores. Once your state sets up a D-SNAP program, you’ll have about a week to apply. If you qualify, you’ll receive benefits within three days.</p>
      <p>Following a disaster, you may lose work or face big expenses, like repairing your home. So even if your normal household income wouldn’t qualify you for regular <a href="https://www.usa.gov/food-help#item-35787">SNAP</a> (food stamp) benefits, you may qualify for D-SNAP. If you already receive SNAP, you can apply for D-SNAP if the amount you’d receive is more than you get under SNAP.</p>
      <p>Following a disaster, your children or your entire family may also be able to get <a href="https://fns-prod.azureedge.net/sites/default/files/CN_Disaster.pdf">free meals</a> through the school meals programs.</p> 
     </article>
