           <h1 class="title" id="page-title">Professional Licenses<span class="icon"></span></h1> 
           <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main"> 
            <div class="block-inner shadows clearfix"> 
             <div class="block-inner-inner clearfix"> 
              <div class="content clearfix"> 
               <article class="node node-popular-topic node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky even clearfix" about="/popular-topic/professional-licenses" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document"> 
                <span property="dc:title" content="Professional Licenses" class="rdf-meta element-hidden"></span> 
                <div> 
                 <span class="print-link"></span>
                 <div class="field field-name-field-overview field-type-text-long field-label-above"> 
                  <h2 class="field-label">Overview:&nbsp;</h2> 
                  <div class="field-items"> 
                   <div class="field-item even">
                    <p>The Secretary of State guides you through the process of registering for a professional license. While you can submit a paper application, applying online is both speedy and safe.</p> 
                   </div> 
                  </div> 
                 </div> 
                 <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-above"> 
                  <h2 class="field-label">What You Should Know:&nbsp;</h2> 
                  <div class="field-items"> 
                   <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                    <ul>
                     <li>The Georgia Secretary of State website lists&nbsp;<a href="http://sos.ga.gov/index.php/licensing/online_licensure_applications">all professions that require a license</a>,&nbsp;such as accounting, nursing, and veterinary work.</li> 
                     <li>After your initial application, you'll need to renew your license every 2 years. The Secretary of State website can help you&nbsp;<a href="https://secure.sos.state.ga.us/mylicense/Login.aspx?process=ren">find out when your current license expires</a>.</li> 
                     <li>Physicians, lawyers, insurance agents, pesticide applicators, and real estate agents are not licensed by the Secretary of State but rather with the state boards that directly oversee those professions.</li> 
                     <li>Find the <a href="http://www.sos.ga.gov/plb/">latest news</a> on professional licensing boards on the Secretary of State website.</li> 
                    </ul>
                   </div> 
                  </div> 
                 </div> 
                 <div class="field-collection-container clearfix">
                  <div class="field field-name-field-faq field-type-field-collection field-label-above"> 
                   <h2 class="field-label">FAQs:&nbsp;</h2> 
                   <div class="field-items"> 
                    <div class="field-item even">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/840"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">Can I practice in Georgia with a professional license from another state?</h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <p>Your out-of-state license does not qualify you to work in Georgia. However, if the standards for getting a license in your old home state are roughly equal to or more difficult than the Georgia standards, you're eligible to apply for a license. Learn more about this by <a href="http://sos.ga.gov/index.php/licensing/online_licensure_applications">visiting your profession's licensing board.</a></p> 
                         </div> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                      </div> 
                     </div>
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item odd">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/841"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">What should I do if I let my professional license lapse?</h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <p>You'll have to request reinstatement of your license. <a href="http://sos.ga.gov/index.php/licensing/online_licensure_applications">Visit your profession's licensing board</a> to find out more.</p> 
                         </div> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                      </div> 
                     </div>
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full field-collection-view-final">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/2201"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">Where can I find information about dental and pharmaceutical licenses? </h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <p>To learn about dental and&nbsp;pharmaceutical&nbsp;license requirements or to learn how to renew a license, consult the&nbsp;<a href="http://gbd.georgia.gov/">Georgia Board of Dentistry</a>&nbsp;(GBD) and the&nbsp;<a href="http://gbp.georgia.gov/">Georgia Board of Pharmacy</a>&nbsp;(GBP).&nbsp;During the 2013 legislative session, the legislature approved&nbsp;<a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20132014/HB/132">House Bill 132</a>, moving the GBD and GBP&nbsp;from the <a href="http://sos.ga.gov/">Secretary of State's</a> office to the <a href="http://dch.georgia.gov/">Department of Community Health</a>.</p> 
                         </div> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                      </div> 
                     </div>
                    </div> 
                   </div> 
                  </div> 
                 </div>
                 <div class="field field-name-field-legal-disclaimer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                  <div class="field-items"> 
                   <div class="field-item even">
                    <p>Source: Secretary of State and Department of Community Health. This information was prepared as a public service of the State of Georgia to provide general information, not to advise on any specific legal problem. It is not, and cannot be construed to be, legal advice. If you have questions regarding any matter contained on this page, please speak with the agency that is the source of the information.</p> 
                   </div>
