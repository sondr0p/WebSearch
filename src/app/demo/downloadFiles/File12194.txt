           <h1 class="title" id="page-title">Child Support<span class="icon"></span></h1> 
           <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main"> 
            <div class="block-inner shadows clearfix"> 
             <div class="block-inner-inner clearfix"> 
              <div class="content clearfix"> 
               <article class="node node-popular-topic node-promoted node-published node-not-sticky odd clearfix" about="/popular-topic/child-support" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document"> 
                <span property="dc:title" content="Child Support" class="rdf-meta element-hidden"></span> 
                <div> 
                 <span class="print-link"></span>
                 <div class="field field-name-field-overview field-type-text-long field-label-above"> 
                  <h2 class="field-label">Overview:&nbsp;</h2> 
                  <div class="field-items"> 
                   <div class="field-item even">
                    <p>The Department of Human Services' Division of Child Support Services helps you collect or pay child support.</p> 
                   </div> 
                  </div> 
                 </div> 
                 <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-above"> 
                  <h2 class="field-label">What You Should Know:&nbsp;</h2> 
                  <div class="field-items"> 
                   <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                    <ul>
                     <li>Before opening a child support case, <strong>collect any relevant documents</strong> — your children's birth certificates and Social Security cards, court custody orders, proof of income, and contact information for both parents.</li> 
                     <li>Private agencies may claim to help you collect child support, but many of these will charge you a fee. Be suspicious of them. The Division of Child Support Services offers assistance <strong>at little or no cost</strong>, and you'll receive 100% of child support payments owed to your family.</li> 
                    </ul>
                   </div> 
                  </div> 
                 </div> 
                 <div class="field-collection-container clearfix">
                  <div class="field field-name-field-faq field-type-field-collection field-label-above"> 
                   <h2 class="field-label">FAQs:&nbsp;</h2> 
                   <div class="field-items"> 
                    <div class="field-item even">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/654"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">How do I file for child support?</h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <ol>
                           <li><span id="docs-internal-guid-d60008f7-cc12-4d9f-dbea-49b87f2871be">Parents and </span><a href="https://dhs.georgia.gov/applying-child-support-kinship-caregiver">kinship caregivers</a> can <a href="https://services.georgia.gov/dhr/cspp/do/public/ApplyNow">apply online</a> with the Division of Child Support Services. You can pay the $25 fee by credit card, money order, or personal check.</li> 
                           <li><strong>Find the parent</strong> who doesn't have custody. Where does that parent live and work?</li> 
                           <li>If you're a mother, confirm that <strong>the father is legally recognized</strong> as a parent. If you aren't sure who your child's father is, Child Support Services will help you set up an appointment for genetic testing.</li> 
                           <li>File a support order.</li> 
                          </ol>
                          <p>Kinship caregivers can also apply for child support. Learn about&nbsp;<a href="https://dhs.georgia.gov/applying-child-support-kinship-caregiver">applying for child support as a kinship caregiver</a>&nbsp;from the Kinship Care Portal.</p> 
                         </div> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                      </div> 
                     </div>
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item odd">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/655"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">How do I receive child support payments?</h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <p>You can <a href="http://ocse.dhr.georgia.gov/portal/site/DHS-OCSE/menuitem.f3ca900e75789bd18e738510da1010a0/?vgnextoid=757a10ad92000010VgnVCM100000bf01010aRCRD">receive payments</a> through either a debit card or direct deposit.</p> 
                         </div> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                      </div> 
                     </div>
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/656"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">How do I pay child support?</h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <p>In Georgia, the <a href="https://childsupport.georgia.gov/family-support-registry">Family Support Registry</a> typically pulls payments directly from your paycheck. However, you can send your own payments online through your bank account, your credit card, or Western Union. You may also send your payments by mail to the address provided in your court order.</p> 
                         </div> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                      </div> 
                     </div>
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item odd">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/1326"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">How do I change the amount of child support due?</h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <p>Both parents have the right to ask DCSS to review a child support order 3 years after the order becomes effective, unless either parent can show substantial change in circumstances for orders less than 3 years old. The request must be made in writing to the child support office handling your case.</p> 
                         </div> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                      </div> 
                     </div>
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even">
                     <div class="field-collection-view clearfix view-mode-full field-collection-view-final">
                      <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-faq clearfix" about="/field-collection/field-faq/1331"> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-question field-type-text field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <h3 class="field-item even">What happens when the non-custodial parent doesn't pay child support?</h3>
                         <h3> </h3>
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <div class="field field-name-field-faq-answer field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"> 
                        <div class="field-items"> 
                         <div class="field-item even">
                          <p>When the non-custodial parent does not pay the full amount or does not pay at all, enforcement action is necessary.</p> 
                         </div>
