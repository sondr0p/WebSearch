          <h1> Amending Your Library's Selection Profile </h1> 
          <ul class="actions"> 
           <li class="print-icon"> <a href="/requirements-guidance/guidance/10-amending-your-library-s-selection-profile?tmpl=component&amp;print=1" title="Print article < Amending Your Library's Selection Profile >" onclick="window.open(this.href,'win2','status=no,toolbar=no,scrollbars=yes,titlebar=no,menubar=no,resizable=yes,width=640,height=480,directories=no,location=no'); return false;" rel="nofollow"> <img src="/media/system/images/printButton.png" alt="Print"> </a> </li>

          <p class="lead">FDLP libraries choose materials for their libraries through selection profiles, that are the list of item numbers for specific desired content. As content and format needs of the community change, so too should the selections of government publications.</p> 
          <div class="alert alert-info">
           NOTE: The addition of tangible items for distribution must be done before October 1 each year. To ensure distribution of tangible materials, submit changes to your library's selection profile before that date. Online only (EL) selection additions take effect immediately.
          </div> 
          <p>A selection profile is the list of item numbers representing the Federal material selected through the FDLP. GPO does not distribute publications by subject; instead GPO makes publications available for selection based on provenance. The <a href="/requirements-guidance/guidance/19-item-number-system" title="Link to the Item Number System guidance">Item Number System</a> is how GPO groups materials together that are produced by individual government agencies or sub-agencies.</p> 
          <h2 data-type="anchor" id="making-changes-to-your-library's-selection-profile"><a name="effective-dates"></a>Making Changes to Your Library's Selection Profile</h2> 
          <p>Changes to selection profiles are made by logging into the <a href="http://selections.fdlp.gov/" target="_blank" title="Link to the Depository Selection Information Management System (DSIMS)">Depository Selection Information Management System (DSIMS)</a>. Use your depository library number and internal password to login. More information about the internal password is located in <a href="/requirements-guidance/instructions/377-passwords-used-in-depository-libraries" title="Link to Passwords Used by Depository Libraries guidance">Passwords Used by Depository Libraries</a>.</p> 
          <p>If an item number is dropped by mistake, contact GPO as soon as possible through <a href="http://www.gpo.gov/askgpo/" target="_blank" title="Link to askGPO">askGPO</a>. Otherwise, GPO may cease sending the publications until October 1, and may not be able to honor claims.</p> 
          <h3>Adding Item Numbers</h3> 
          <p>Selectors can add item numbers to selection profiles throughout the year. When a change is made, the selection profile will immediately reflect the addition of item numbers corresponding to online formats (EL). Item numbers corresponding to tangible formats (e.g., paper (P), tangible electronic (E), microfiche (MF), multiple format (P/EL, MF/EL, E/EL)) will be added to your Selection Cart in DSIMS. The addition(s) and distribution of tangibles will take effect and begin as of October 1.</p> 
          <h3>Dropping Item Numbers</h3> 
          <p>Item numbers can be dropped any time during the year, with changes taking effect immediately. Microfiche requests take longer to process because microfiche is produced and shipped by the contractor. After submitting a request to drop microfiche, it can take up to a week for the drop to take effect. &nbsp;Contact Library Services and Content Management (LSCM) via askGPO if you continue to receive microfiche.</p> 
          <p>When an item number is dropped, all material previously received under the item number must still be retained for the statutory five-year retention period, unless superseded or officially substituted. When five years have passed, libraries can offer titles on a disposal list to their regional library.</p> 
          <p>After dropping an item number, a library may not submit a claim for a publication with that item number.</p> 
          <h2 data-type="anchor" id="reviewing-your-library's-selection-profile">Reviewing Your Library's Selection Profile</h2> 
          <p>GPO recommends that libraries review their selections regularly, keeping the U.S. Government information needs of their community in mind. To view your librarys current selection profile, consult the <a href="/requirements-guidance-2/instructions/34-item-lister" title="Link to Item Lister guidance">Item Lister</a>. For more information about item numbers, see the article on <a href="http://fdlp.gov/requirements-guidance-2/instructions/281-list-of-classes" title="Link to the List of Classes guidance">List of Classes</a>.</p> 
          <p>How a library goes about reviewing and selecting item numbers is a local decision. Some libraries divide up the List of Classes among their subject experts; others have the depository coordinator responsible for selecting all Federal material on behalf of the library. Selectors may use <a href="/webtech-notes" title="Link to WEBTech Notes">WEBTech Notes</a> to locate newly created item numbers that could be added to the librarys selection profile. <a href="http://govdoc.wichita.edu/ddm2/gdocframes.asp" target="_blank" title="Link to Documents Data Miner 2">Documents Data Miner 2</a> is a non-GPO resource that combines selection information and the List of Classes and may assist in the item selection process as well. To avoid confusion, it is recommends that one person manage the profile in DSIMS; typically this is the depository coordinator.</p> 
          <h2>Online (EL) Item Numbers Selection</h2> 
          <p>While online materials are not distributed, and there is no requirement for libraries to select them, there are several reasons to select online (EL) item numbers.</p> 
          <div class="alert alert-info">
           Please note: While most (EL) online item numbers are for online only, there is a small group of item numbers that include both online and tangible formats. If you select one of these multiple format item numbers, the library will receive multiple formats (ex: P/EL, MF/EL).
          </div> 
          <ul> 
           <li>To ensure that a library does not miss out on online records or receipt of tangible publications relevant to the librarys collection, GPO may create new item numbers and map them to already existing item numbers. Additionally, GPO may add the new numbers to your item selection profile. That is because GPO assumes that libraries want to receive new publications similar to those already selected. Libraries that do not want to select these newly added item numbers should drop them immediately. New item numbers appear in <a href="http://fdlp.gov/requirements-guidance-2/instructions/38-webtech-notes" title="Link to WEBTech Notes guidance">WEBTech Notes</a>.</li> 
           <li>Including online item numbers in your selection profile may aid bibliographic record vendors in including records for those online items in the librarys record load.</li> 
           <li>By using the Locate in a Library feature on the Catalog of U.S. Government Publications (CGP), researchers can locate libraries that provide access to free Agency Subscription Databases provided through the Federal Depository Library Program.</li> 
           <li>There are various tools that have been developed to help you understand your depository item selection profile in its regional and national context. GPO manages the <a href="/file-repository/collection-management/union-list-of-item-selections" title="Link to Union List of Item Selections (UNION-L)">Union List of Item Selections (UNION-L)</a> to facilitate community-created tools such as <a href="http://govdoc.wichita.edu/ddm2/gdocframes.asp" target="_blank" title="Link to Documents Data Miner 2 (DDM2)">Documents Data Miner 2 (DDM2)</a>. Data about item selections at the local, regional, and national level assist depository staff in efficiently developing their collections, while also determining the collection strengths of other depositories.</li> 
           <li>Libraries may compare their selection profile with new online publications and their associated catalog records using the <a href="http://catalog.gpo.gov/F/?func=file&amp;file_name=find-net" title="Link to the New Electronic Titles List (NET) report">New Electronic Titles List (NET)</a> in the CGP.</li> 
           <li>Formats associated with item numbers may change at the request of the issuing agency. For example, if an agency discontinues posting an online publication, GPO may distribute the publication (whole issues or parts not available online) in a tangible format. When this happens, libraries that select the EL item number would receive the tangible version without missing any new publications or issues.</li> 
          </ul> 
          <h4>FDLP Collection Lists</h4> 
          <p>There are several collection lists that library staff may want to review, in developing their collections. The titles on these lists are U.S. Government resources that may be of interest to depository library communities. Please note: There is no requirement to select any titles on these lists in any format.</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li><a href="/requirements-guidance/collections-and-databases/1442-basic-collection" title="Link to Basic Collection List">Basic Collection List</a></li> 
           <li><a href="/requirements-guidance-2/collections-and-databases/267-suggested-core-collections" title="Suggested Core Collections">Suggested Core Collection List</a></li> 
           <li><a href="/requirements-guidance/collections-and-databases/1443-essential-titles#essential-titles-list" title="Link to Essential Titles List for Public Use in Paper or Other Tangible Format">Essential Titles List for Public Use in Paper or Other Tangible Format</a></li> 
          </ul> 
          <h4>Agency Subscription Databases</h4> 
          <p>Federal depository libraries have free access to select Federal databases that are otherwise subscription based. To gain access to these resources, complete the request form. &nbsp;The library should add the item numbers for the databases to the item selection profile. See <a href="http://fdlp.gov/requirements-guidance-2/collections-and-databases/41-depository-library-no-fee-access-to-agency-subscription-services" title="Link to Agency Subscription Databases">Agency Subscription Databases</a> for more information and links to the forms.</p> 
          <h4>Restrictions on Format Selections</h4> 
          <p>Selective depository libraries may only select one tangible format of a publication. Of course, selectives can always select the online equivalents (if available). For example, librarians at a selective library may choose a print version of a publication and also provide access to an online version. &nbsp;Alternatively, they may select a microfiche version and also provide access to the online version. They may not, however, select both the print and microfiche format. On the other hand, regional depository libraries may select multiple tangible formats, and are exempt from this requirement.</p> 
          <h4>Restrictions on Item Numbers by Library Type</h4> 
          <p>Only specific library types can select certain item numbers. The List of Classes title field contains a note that describes any selection limitations. Some examples follow:</p> 
          <p>Only regional depository libraries may select the following:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Item number <strong>0993-A-01</strong>, Congressional Record (bound) 1985 and forward, SuDocs Class X 1.1:</li> 
           <li>Item number <strong>1008-F</strong>, Serial Set (bound) 105th Congress and forward, SuDocs Class Y 1.1/2:</li> 
          </ul> 
          <p>Only regional depository libraries and law libraries may select the following:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Item number <strong>0740-A-02</strong>, Supreme Court Decisions (decisions in individual cases) (slip opinions), SuDocs Class JU 6.8/B:</li> 
          </ul> 
          <p>Refer to item numbers in the List of Classes to determine any restrictions on selection.</p> 
          <h4>Maintaining a History of the Library's Selection Profile for Collection Development and Management</h4> 
          <p>Understanding past selection and retention decisions may help library staff understand what is in the depository collection and guide future selection decisions. Therefore, libraries should retain historical files of their selection profiles. Historical information may come in useful for retrospective cataloging projects as well as disposal projects. There are various ways to record your selections. If the library has retained historic GPO-issued item number cards, once issued to identify library selections, consider retaining them. Also, a library you may choose to archive Item Lister files or maintain a local selection profile database.</p> 
          <p>Ideally, library staff will document item selection over time in a collection development policy.</p> 
          <h4>Tools Used when Working with Selection Profiles</h4> 
          <ul> 
           <li><a href="http://catalog.gpo.gov/" target="_blank" title="Link to the Catalog of U.S. Government Publications (CGP)">Catalog of U.S. Government Publications (CGP)</a> allows searching by item number in the advanced search to identify the distribution frequency of a given item number.</li> 
           <li><a href="http://selections.fdlp.gov/" target="_blank" title="Link to Depository Selection Information Management System (DSIMS)">Depository Selection Information Management System (DSIMS)</a> allows Federal depository libraries to view and manage their selection profiles. At any time, libraries may drop tangible publications, add online (EL) publications, and can also queue additions for tangible items into a cart. At the start of each new fiscal year (October 1), GPO processes the cart.</li> 
           <li><a href="http://selections.fdlp.gov/itemlister" target="_blank" title="Link to Item Lister">Item Lister</a> lists selected and/or non-selected item numbers for one library at a time.</li> 
           <li><a href="http://fdlp.gov/file-repository/collection-management/list-of-classes" title="Link to the List of Classes">List of Classes</a> lists all item numbers available for selection; includes SuDocs stem, title, and format. Associated files provide historical information such as inactive item numbers, inactive SuDocs class stems, and so on.</li> 
           <li><a href="/file-repository/collection-management/union-list-of-item-selections" title="Link to Union List of Item Selections (UNION-L)">Union List of Item Selections (UNION-L)</a> lists current selections of all depository libraries (available in one large file).</li> 
           <li><a href="http://fdlp.gov/component/webtech/" title="Link to WEBTech Notes">WEBTech Notes</a> documents SuDocs class changes, format changes, new item numbers, discontinuance of titles, and so on.</li> 
           <li><a href="http://govdoc.wichita.edu/ddm2/gdocframes.asp" target="_blank" title="Link to Documents Data Miner 2 (DDM2)">Documents Data Miner 2 (DDM2)</a> is a non-GPO resource that identifies selections and non-selections; search by item number in Shipping Lists or the Shelflist sections to identify the distribution frequency of a given item number. Some bulk exporting is available.</li> 
           <li><em>Guide to U.S. Government Publications</em> (formerly commonly known as Andriot, the last name of the former editor) is a non-GPO annual publication currently published by Gale. This annotated publication outlines current and former Superintendent of Documents classification assignments and their associated item numbers.</li> 
          </ul>
