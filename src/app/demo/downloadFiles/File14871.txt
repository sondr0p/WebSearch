           <h1>Accessibility</h1> 
           <p>All National Archives and Records Administration (NARA) museums, research facilities, and Presidential Libraries are accessible (<a href="https://www.ada.gov/">ADA compliant</a>). If you have questions regarding a specific location’s accessibility, please <a href="/locations/">contact the&nbsp;facility</a> directly.</p> 
           <p><a href="/eeo/policy/508-aba-rights">Notice of Rights Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, as amended (29 U.S.C. § 794d and the Architectural Barriers Act of 1968 (42 U.S.C. §§ 4151--57) )</a></p> 
           <h2><a name="services"></a>Disability Services</h2> 
           <p><strong>Blind and Low Vision Visitors</strong><br> NARA’s museum has five copies of the <a href="/founding-docs">Constitution and Declaration of Independence</a> in braille. Please contact visitor services for more information at <a href="mailto:visitorservices@nara.gov">visitorservices@nara.gov</a> or call 202-357-6816.</p> 
           <p><strong>Deaf/Hard of Hearing Visitors</strong><br> Most research facilities&nbsp;and all Presidential Libraries provide Interpretypes® as a quick and easy means of face-to-face communication between deaf, hard of hearing, and hearing people. These can be used for simple research questions, visitor Q &amp; A, and general information. Interpretypes® will not be replacing interpreters, but are a means of immediate access for those with hearing loss. For information about interpreted museum tours, please email<a href="mailto:visitorservices@nara.gov"> visitorservices@nara.gov</a> or call 202-357-6816.</p> 
           <p>Sign language interpreting and CART (Communication Access in Real Time) services are available with a seven business day notice. Please <a href="/locations/">contact the facility</a> in which you are interested in visiting for more information about reserving services.</p> 
           <p><a href="/museum/visit/accessibility.html#video_tour">Welcome video tour of the National Archives Museum in American Sign Language (ASL)</a>.</p> 
           <p><strong>Personal Assistant Services (PAS) information</strong><br> <a href="/eeo/policy/personal-assistance-services">NARA provides personal assistant services (PAS) </a>to NARA employees with targeted disabilities who require assistance with basic activities of daily living such as&nbsp;eating, removing and putting on clothing, and using the restroom&nbsp;unless doing so will impose an undue hardship on the Agency.&nbsp; Personal assistance services differ from <em>medical</em> services and services that are typically performed by someone who often has the job title of “personal assistant.” Personal assistance services are <em>non-medical services</em> that allow individuals with targeted disabilities to perform basic human activities of daily living such as eating, removing and putting on clothing, and using the restroom. A full list of targeted disabilities can be found in NARA 303, Processing Reasonable Accommodation Requests for Employees and Applicants with Disabilities, Section 303.4m.&nbsp; Personal assistance services are not the same as services that help the individuals perform job-related tasks, such as filing, or using a computer.</p> 
           <p>Read the&nbsp;<a href="https://update.archives.gov/eeo/policy/nara-303">NARA 303 - Processing Reasonable Accommodation Requests for Employees and Applicants with Disabilities</a>.</p> 
           <p><strong>Reasonable Accommodation Procedures</strong><br> NARA is committed to providing reasonable accommodations to its employees and applicants for employment to ensure that individuals with disabilities enjoy full access to equal employment opportunity at NARA. <a href="/eeo/policy/processing-reasonable-accommodation-requests">Access the policy</a>.<strong>&nbsp;</strong></p> 
           <p><strong>Service Animals</strong><br> NARA has a Service Animal Policy, which permits animals specifically trained to perform tasks for persons with disabilities, access to public areas. Therapy animals are not considered service animals under the ADA Amendments Act of 2008. Service animals must be under the control of their handlers at all times.</p> 
           <p><strong>Wheelchair Users</strong><br> Wheelchairs are provided on a first-come, first-serve basis.&nbsp;</p> 
           <h2>Information Communication Technology</h2> 
           <p>The National Archives and Records Administration (NARA) is committed to making its electronic and information technologies accessible to the widest possible audience, including individuals with disabilities. NARA regularly monitors its sites to ensure that they meet and exceed the requirements of <a href="#learn">Section 508</a> of the <a href="http://www.access-board.gov/508.htm">Rehabilitation Act</a> (29 U.S.C. 794d), as amended in 1998, and the <a href="http://www.w3.org/WAI/intro/wcag.php">W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines</a>.</p>

           <h2><a id="alt" name="alt"></a>Alternate Access to Web Information</h2> 
           <p>If the format of any material on our website interferes with your ability to access the information, please contact us at the address above.</p> 
           <p>To allow us to respond in a manner most helpful to you, please let us know:</p> 
           <ol> 
            <li>Nature of your accessibility issue.</li> 
            <li>Preferred format in which to receive the material.</li> 
            <li>Web address (URL) of the material with which you are having difficulty.</li> 
            <li>Your contact information.</li> 
           </ol> 
           <p>Documents on our website are in many formats. The primary file types we use are.</p> 
           <h3>HTML and Images</h3> 
           <p>Generally, we use Hypertext Markup Language (HTML). In creating HTML documents, we follow Section 508 standards; for example, we include alternate text to describe graphics.</p> 
           <h3><a name="shatpdf"></a> <a id="adobe" name="adobe"></a>Adobe Acrobat Reader and PDF Files<i class="fa fa-file-pdf-o"></i></h3> 
           <p>Our website also contains documents in Adobe Portable Document Format ® (PDF). Most PDF files will be accessible to those who use assistive technology such as screen readers. Some legacy or software-created PDF files may not be accessible. If you are experiencing difficulty reading any PDF file, please <a href="#coordinator">contact us</a>, and we will send you an alternate version.</p> 
           <ul> 
            <li>PDF files require the <a href="http://www.adobe.com/products/reader/">free Adobe Acrobat Reader</a>.</li> 
            <li>Adobe also provides <a href="http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/access_onlinetools.html">free PDF tools for the visually impaired</a>.</li> 
            <li><a href="http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/readermain.html">Download the free Adobe Acrobat Reader</a>.</li> 
           </ul> 
           <p>For detailed, up-to-date information about Acrobat Reader or the PDF format, visit <a href="http://www.acrobat.com">Adobe Systems Inc.</a></p> 
           <h3><a id="power" name="power"></a>PowerPoint Files<i class="fa fa-file-powerpoint-o"></i></h3> 
           <p>You can view files that are in Microsoft PowerPoint ® (PPT) format if you have any version of PowerPoint installed on your computer.</p> 
           <ul> 
            <li>Microsoft Corporation offers a <a href="http://www.adobe.com/products/reader/">free PowerPoint file viewer.</a></li> 
            <li>Download and get help using the <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=13">PowerPoint Viewer</a> at the Microsoft Download Center.</li> 
           </ul> 
           <h3><a id="word" name="power"></a> Word Files<i class="fa fa-file-word-o"></i></h3> 
           <p>You can view files in Microsoft Word® (DOC) format if you have Word, version 6.0 or higher, installed on your computer. Word documents can also be viewed with many other word-processing software programs.</p> 
           <ul> 
            <li>Microsoft Corporation offers a <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=4">free Word file viewer</a> at the Microsoft Download Center.</li> 
           </ul> 
           <h2><a id="learn" name="learn"></a> Learn More</h2> 
           <p>Section 508 is a Federal law that requires agencies to provide individuals with disabilities equal access to electronic information and data comparable to those who do not have disabilities unless an undue burden would be imposed on the agency. The Section 508 standards are the technical requirements and criteria that are used to measure conformance within this law. More information on Section 508 and the technical standards can be found on the following sites:</p> 
           <ul> 
            <li><a href="http://www.section508.gov/index.cfm">Section508.gov</a> provides Federal employees and the public with access to resources for understanding and implementing the requirements of Section 508, including Section 508 tools, resources, standards, and news.</li> 
            <li>The <a href="http://www.access-board.gov/508.htm">U.S. Access Board's Section 508 Homepage</a> provides information on Section 508 law, frequently asked questions, and standards.</li> 
            <li>The <a href="http://www.w3.org/WAI/">Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI) homepage</a> provides strategies, guidelines, and resources to make the Web accessible to people with disabilities.</li> 
           </ul>
