          <h1> Cataloging Record Distribution Program </h1> 
          <ul class="actions"> 
           <li class="print-icon"> <a href="/catalogingandclassification/cataloging-record-distribution-program?tmpl=component&amp;print=1" title="Print article < Cataloging Record Distribution Program >" onclick="window.open(this.href,'win2','status=no,toolbar=no,scrollbars=yes,titlebar=no,menubar=no,resizable=yes,width=640,height=480,directories=no,location=no'); return false;" rel="nofollow"> <img src="/media/system/images/printButton.png" alt="Print"> </a> </li> 
           <li class="email-icon"> <a href="/component/mailto/?tmpl=component&amp;template=fdlp&amp;link=5e7c42fcc0389032d455f4ad1e788bf4b3e2d5d1" title="Email this link to a friend" onclick="window.open(this.href,'win2','width=400,height=450,menubar=yes,resizable=yes'); return false;" rel="nofollow"> <img src="/media/system/images/emailButton.png" alt="Email"> </a> </li> 
          </ul> 
          <dl class="article-info"> 
           <dt class="article-info-term">
            Details
           </dt> 
           <dd class="modified">
             Last Updated: May 29 2019 
           </dd> 
           <dd class="published">
             Published: October 24 2012 
           </dd> 
          </dl> 
          <p class="lead">The Cataloging Records Distribution Program (CRDP) provides&nbsp;<a title="link to GPO’s Cataloging Data in the Cataloging Record Distribution Program" href="/catalogingandclassification/gpo-s-cataloging-data-in-the-cataloging-record-distribution-program">GPO produced catalog records</a> to participating Federal depository libraries at no cost to the libraries.</p> 
          <p>The U.S. Government Publishing Office (<a target="_blank" title="Link to US Government Publishing Office Site" href="https://www.gpo.gov" rel="noopener noreferrer">GPO</a>) started the CRDP on October 1, 2009 and has expanded the program each year. Currently, it serves up to 185 Federal depository libraries.</p> 
          <p>GPO contracts with <a target="_blank" title="Link to Marcive.com" href="http://home.marcive.com/cataloging-record-distribution-project" rel="noopener noreferrer">MARCIVE, Inc</a>. for bibliographic record services for CRDP participants. MARCIVE personnel work with each participating library to develop a CRDP bibliographic records profile, and they provide monthly sets of GPO produced bibliographic records for each library, based on their individually customized profiles.</p> 
          <p>Libraries add the records to their own library catalogs, enhancing public access and awareness of U.S. Government publications. Libraries do not pay to participate; however, they must follow minimum participation requirements in order to remain in the program.</p> 
          <p><a href="/requirements-guidance/instructions/4006-cataloging-record-distribution-program-faqs-for-current-participants" title="Link to FAQ page" target="_blank" rel="noopener"> <button class="btn btn-primary" type="button" title="Link to Register to attend virtually">FAQs for current participants</button> </a></p> 
          <h3 data-type="anchor" id="benefits"><a name="benefits">Benefits of the CRDP</a></h3> 
          <p>The advantages to participation are many, including:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>The service is available at no cost to participating Federal depository libraries.</li> 
           <li><a title="link to GPO’s Cataloging Data in the Cataloging Record Distribution Program" href="/catalogingandclassification/gpo-s-cataloging-data-in-the-cataloging-record-distribution-program">Quality bibliographic records</a>&nbsp;are provided monthly.</li> 
           <li>Participating libraries profiled to get current bibliographic records can make changes anytime to their FDLP item number selection profiles and will automatically get bibliographic records the next month based on the updated item number selection profiles.</li> 
           <li>Libraries may configure the bibliographic record files received.</li> 
           <li>The monthly process for acquiring the records sets is easy.</li> 
           <li>Receiving bibliographic records reduces library staff cataloging time.</li> 
           <li>Participants have consistently praised CRDP customer service.</li> 
           <li>Library staff may request a referral to a CRDP Buddy, a knowledgeable librarian at another CRDP participating library who has offered to help consult about the CRDP.</li> 
          </ul> 
          <h3 data-type="anchor" id="how_to_join"><a name="how_to_join">How to Join the CRDP</a></h3> 
          <p>Participation in the CRDP is “first come, first served".</p> 
          <p>Contact GPO’s Office of Outreach and Support if you are interested in learning about any program vacancies. Ask for Kathy Bayer or Lara Flint.</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Phone: <a href="tel:202-512-1119">202-512-1119</a> (shared phone line)</li> 
           <li>Electronic:<a target="_blank" title="Link to Outreach and Support Contact Form" href="/component/weblinks/weblink/390-forms/4-education-and-outreach-contact-form?Itemid=101&amp;task=weblink.go" rel="noopener noreferrer">Email Outreach and Support Form</a></li> 
          </ul> 
          <p>The CRDP has proven to be so successful that GPO typically has a waiting list for participation. Libraries on wait lists are typically invited to join after the beginning of the next Federal fiscal year (October 1st). If a program vacancy arises during the fiscal year, the next library on the waiting list is able to join the program at that time.</p> 
          <p>If your depository library is interested in joining, you are encouraged to:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Review the CRDP minimum participation requirements to make sure your library will be able to fulfill the requirements.</li> 
           <li>Review the <a target="_blank" title="Link to CRDP Profile Guide" href="http://www.fdlp.gov/file-repository/1664-cataloging-record-distribution-project-crdp-profile-guide" rel="noopener noreferrer">CRDP Profile Guide</a>, which provides instructions that libraries joining the CRDP follow to notify MARCIVE how they would like their records sets customized.</li> 
           <li>Review and update the depository’s FDLP item number selection profile, as appropriate.</li> 
          </ul> 
          <div class="alert alert-info"> 
           <p>Once GPO invites your library to join the CRDP:</p> 
           <ol> 
            <li>Review the following: 
             <ul> 
              <li>&nbsp;<a title="Link to CRDP Profile Guide document" class="doclink" href="/file-repository/1664-cataloging-record-distribution-project-crdp-profile-guide">CRDP Profile Guide</a></li> 
              <li><a title="Link to Joining the CRDP document" href="/file-repository/about-the-fdlp/gpo-projects/2756-steps-to-begin-crdp-9-14-2016">Joining the CRDP</a> A short outline of steps to take when joining the CRDP and what you can expect once your library is an active participant</li> 
              <li>A <a href="http://login.icohere.com/connect/d_connect_itemframer.cfm?vsDTTitle=Completing%20the%20CRDP%20Profile&amp;dseq=18332&amp;dtseq=103945&amp;emdisc=2&amp;mkey=public1172&amp;vbDTA=0&amp;viNA=0&amp;vsDTA=&amp;PAN=2&amp;bDTC=0&amp;topictype=standard%20default%20linear&amp;vsSH=A" title="Link to Completing the CRDP Profile Guide" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">webcast </a>about completion of the CRDP Profile Guide&nbsp;</li> 
             </ul> </li> 
            <li>Submit the CRDP Profile. Access the form through the link in the first paragraph of the CRDP Profile Guide.</li> 
           </ol>
          </div> 
          <h3 data-type="anchor" id="part_req"><a name="participation_requirements">Participation Requirements</a></h3> 
          <p>Since GPO pays for participating libraries to receive bibliographic records through the CRDP, these libraries are required to:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li><strong>Maintain CRDP point(s) of contact with both GPO and MARCIVE, Inc.</strong> A library must always have at least one current point of contact for this program. Notify both GPO and MARCIVE of any changes in the points of contact.</li> 
           <li><strong>Retrieve catalog records each month.</strong> Records are typically made available for two weeks at the end of the second week of the month. 
            <ul> 
             <li>Failure to see the email notification from MARCIVE about availability of records or to be contacted by GPO about record pick-up does not excuse a library from picking up records. If you haven’t seen a notification by the middle of the month, you should get in touch with MARCIVE to inquire about records availability.</li> 
             <li>If you miss picking up records during the two-week window, you may contact MARCIVE to request reposting. This may be done only once during a fiscal year and must be done by the end of the month that the records were made available.</li> 
            </ul> </li> 
           <li><strong>Load the records into the ILS/OPAC on a timely basis</strong>, which is preferably within two weeks but at least before the next set of records is available the following month, and test the results.</li> 
           <li><strong>Profile to receive at least ten records each month, on average.</strong></li> 
           <li><strong>Complete an annual GPO survey about the CRDP.</strong></li> 
           <li>Have an integrated library system which supports Resource Description and Access (RDA) bibliographic records.</li> 
           <li>(For new participants) After getting an invitation to join, complete the initial CRDP Profile and testing within three weeks.</li> 
          </ul> 
          <p>Libraries with OCLC WMS may set up their CRDP Profiles so they do not need to pick up or load records.</p> 
          <div class="alert alert-error"> 
           <p><strong>PLEASE NOTE</strong>: GPO monitors non-compliance with these requirements and reserves the right to remove libraries from the CRDP when they do not fulfill the requirements.</p> 
          </div> 
          <p>If your library is uncertain about meeting the participation requirements, please note that GPO <a title="Link to CRDP page" href="/catalogingandclassification/sources-of-gpo-cataloging-records">catalog records are available through other sources</a>.</p> 
          <p>To get a sense of how many bibliographic records your library may get each month based on your current FDLP item number selection profile, you may search the section entitled “Catalog”, filtered by your depository library number, in <a target="_blank" title="Link to DDM2" href="http://govdoc.wichita.edu/ddm2/startup.asp" rel="noopener noreferrer">Documents Data Miner 2</a>.</p> 
          <h3 data-type="anchor" id="crdp_profile"><a name="crdp_profile">CRDP Profile</a></h3> 
          <p>Participating libraries create a CRDP Profile to describe the types of records they wish to receive and how they will be organized in order to meet the specifications of the local library system.</p> 
          <p>See the <a target="_blank" title="Link to Profile Guide" href="http://www.fdlp.gov/file-repository/1664-cataloging-record-distribution-project-crdp-profile-guide" rel="noopener noreferrer">Profile Guide</a>, which has instructions for a new library joining the CRDP and helpful information to reference in case participating libraries wish to update or change their current CRDP Profiles.</p> 
          <p>Each library participant may select one of the following options:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>All newly created cataloging records</li> 
           <li>All new records, plus changed or updated bibliographic records</li> 
          </ul> 
          <div class="alert alert-danger">
           Please note that new Historic Shelflist records are not currently available in the CRDP. GPO is not currently creating any of these records at this time, 
           <a href="/news-and-events/3957-historic-shelflist-transcription-update" title="Link to Historic Shelflist Transcription Update News Alert" target="_blank" rel="noopener">pending review of the Historic Shelflist transcription project</a>.
          </div> 
          <div class="alert alert-info"> 
           <h5>Libraries joining the CRDP</h5> 
           <p>Libraries joining the CRDP set up their CRDP Profile through the form linked from the CRDP Profile Guide. &nbsp;<br>New libraries in the CRDP may make two cataloging specification updates within the fiscal year they join. GPO’s fiscal year runs from October 1st through September 30th.</p> 
          </div> 
          <div class="alert alert-info"> 
           <h5>Current CRDP participants</h5> 
           <p>Participating libraries contact MARCIVE, Inc. directly to make changes to their CRDP Profiles. <br>Each continuing CRDP library is entitled to make up to two cataloging specification updates to the record sets output during each Federal fiscal year. GPO’s fiscal year runs from October 1st through September 30th.<br>Participating libraries, please note: When you update your library’s FDLP item number selection profile or GPO makes changes to item numbers that affect your library’s profile, MARCIVE automatically provides bibliographic records the next month for the updated item numbers selected.&nbsp; There is no need to notify GPO or MARCIVE about item number changes.</p> 
          </div> 
          <h3 data-type="anchor" id="record_customization"><a name="record_customization">Record Customization</a></h3> 
          <p>Record sets may be customized in the CRDP. Libraries may choose from among the following CRDP custom options:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>Organization of records sets. They may be in one file or they can be separated by format, such as serials or online only (EL).</li> 
           <li>Output limited by format, such as online only (EL)</li> 
           <li>Creation of item record holding fields</li> 
           <li>Creation of up to four holding codes</li> 
           <li>Placement of OCLC numbers, SuDocs class numbers, and designated MARC fields according to local practice</li> 
           <li>Record sets in MARC-8 or UTF-8</li> 
           <li>Sending records to OCLC for the purpose of setting holdings</li> 
          </ul> 
          <p>Note that MARCIVE adds the following note in a 500 field, “CRDP Project record”.</p> 
          <h3 data-type="anchor" id="record_distribution"><a name="record_distribution">Record Distribution</a></h3> 
          <p>Records are distributed in batches once a month from MARCIVE, typically at the end of the second week of the month. The records sets are for publications cataloged by GPO during the previous month.</p> 
          <p>CRDP points of contact at libraries are notified by MARCIVE each month by email when their records are available to pick up via FTP or HTTP. The emails include the quantity of records and the location where the records may be retrieved via FTP or HTTP .</p> 
          <p>Once notified, libraries have two weeks to retrieve records. The email notification also includes the expiration date after which the records will not be available.</p> 
          <p>Libraries choosing to leave the CRDP may retain the bibliographic records received through the program</p> 
          <h3 data-type="anchor" id="excluded_services"><a name="excluded_services">Excluded Services</a></h3> 
          <p>The following services are not available through the CRDP:</p> 
          <ul> 
           <li>GPO acquisition records (brief bibs)</li> 
           <li>MARCIVE Shipping List Service (SLS) records</li> 
           <li>Barcodes/smart barcodes</li> 
           <li>Superintendent of Documents (SuDocs) number labels</li> 
           <li>Access to MARCIVEWeb Docs (database offered by MARCIVE)</li> 
           <li>Authority control services</li> 
           <li>Custom reporting</li> 
           <li>MARC records profiled by criteria not in the CRDP</li> 
           <li>Record loads tailored for a consortial catalog</li> 
           <li>Retrospective cataloging</li> 
          </ul>
