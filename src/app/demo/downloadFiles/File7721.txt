           <h1>Veterans' Benefit Resources</h1> 
           <p>A variety of important benefits and services are available to veterans and to their families, survivors and next-of-kin from the <a href="#va">Department of Veterans Affairs</a> and <a href="#other">other agencies</a>.</p> 
           <p>While the National Archives does not provide these benefits, we can help you obtain the <a href="/veterans/military-service-records/index.html">copies of Military Service Records</a> or other proof of service which you'll need to prove eligibility.</p>

           <h3 class="clear"><a name="medical-claims"></a>Medical Benefits and Claims</h3> 
           <p>The <a href="http://www.va.gov/">Department of Veterans Affairs</a> is the primary source of information on Veterans Health Benefits.</p> 
           <p>Veterans who plan to file a claim for medical benefits with the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) do not need to request a copy of their <a href="/veterans/military-service-records/medical-records.html">military health record</a> from the National Archives' NPRC-MPR. The original health records are provided by the Center when requested by the VA after the claim is filed. Many health records were lent to the Department of Veterans Affairs prior to the 1973 fire.</p> 
           <p>Veterans who filed a medical claim should contact the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) in order to determine if their record is already on file. The VA Toll Free # is: 1-800-827-1000 and will connect the caller to the nearest VA office.</p>

           <h3><a id="local" name="local"></a>State and Local Government Veterans Agencies</h3> 
           <p>Many states, counties and local municipalities offer additional veterans benefits and programs. Some may even be able to assist in obtaining copies of military service records.</p>

           <h3>Veterans Service Organizations and Other Groups</h3> 
           <p>A variety of non-profit and private organizations can also help you learn about the services and benefits available to veterans. The following is only a short list. Please see the <a href="http://www1.va.gov/vso/index.cfm">Directory of Veterans Service Organizations</a> from the Department of Veterans Affairs for a comprehensive and up-to-date list.</p> 
           <p class="smallest">(These links are provided for informational purposes only and are not endorsed by the National Archives and Records Administration. <a href="/global-pages/privacy.html#disclaimer">Read our disclaimer of endorsement</a>.)</p> 
           <p><a id="vetorgs" name="vetorgs"></a><strong>Veterans Organizations:</strong></p> 
           <ul> 
            <li><a href="http://www1.va.gov/vso/index.cfm"><strong>Directory of Veterans Service Organizations</strong></a><br> <strong>Updated and Comprehensive Database of Veterans Organizations Compiled by the VA.</strong></li> 
            <li><a href="http://www.dav.org/">Disabled American Veterans (DAV)</a><br> Support organization for veterans, providing a nationwide network of services, free of charge to all veterans and members of their families.</li> 
            <li><a href="http://www.vfw.org">Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW)</a><br> One of the largest veterans organizations, providing veterans' support and community services.</li> 
            <li><a href="http://www.legion.org/">The American Legion</a><br> Site maintained by one of the largest veterans organizations.</li> 
            <li><a href="http://www.jwv.org">Jewish War Veterans of the United States of America</a> - The Jewish War Veterans (JWV) of the USA, organized in 1896 by Jewish Veterans of the Civil War, is the oldest active national veterans' service organization in America.</li> 
            <li><a href="http://www.amvets.org/">AMVETS</a><br> AMVETS provides support for veterans, active military and community services.</li> 
            <li><a href="http://www.vva.org/">Vietnam Veterans of America (VVA)</a><br> Veterans service organization dedicated to helping Vietnam-era veterans</li> 
            <li><strong>Service Branch Organizations</strong> 
             <ul> 
              <li><a href="http://www.ausa.org">U.S. Army - Association of the U.S. Army</a></li> 
              <li><a href="http://www.afa.org">U.S. Air Force - Air Force Association</a></li> 
              <li><a href="http://www.navyleague.org">U.S. Navy - Navy League</a></li> 
              <li><a href="http://www.mcleague.org">U.S. Marine Corps - Marine Corps League</a></li> 
             </ul> </li> 
           </ul>

           <p><a href="http://www.aarp.org/">American Association of Retired Persons (AARP)</a><br> AARP is a nonprofit, nonpartisan membership organization providing information, advocacy and benefits for people age 50 and over.</p>

           <p><a href="http://www.military.com">Military.com</a><br> A commercial site offering free resources to serve, connect, and inform active duty members, reservists, guard members, retirees, veterans, family members, defense workers and those considering military careers.</p> 
           <p><a href="http://www.afcrossroads.com/home.cfm">Armed Forces Crossroads Community</a><br> A commercial site supporting the military community.</p>

The site is free to members and offers an array of resources to help past and present military personnel take advantage of their military benefits.</p>
