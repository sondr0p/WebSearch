        <h1 class="entry-title">Launching Disasters.Data.Gov to Empower First Responders and Survivors with Innovative Tools and Data</h1> 
        <div class="entry-meta" xmlns="//www.w3.org/1999/html"> 
         <time class="published" datetime="2014-12-14T12:00:05-04:00">December 14, 2014&nbsp;&nbsp;<i>By Meredith Lee, Heather King, Brian Forde</i></time> 
        </div> 
       </header> 
       <div class="entry-content"> 
        <p>Strengthening our Nation’s resilience to disasters is a shared responsibility, with all community members contributing their unique skills and perspectives. Whether you’re a data steward who can&nbsp;<a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/call-action-open-data/" class="local-link">unlock information and foster a culture of open data</a>, an innovator who can help&nbsp;<a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/innovator-challenge/" class="local-link">address disaster preparedness challenges</a>, or a volunteer ready to&nbsp;<a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/get-involved/" class="local-link">join the “Innovation for Disasters” movement</a>, we are excited for you to visit the new <a href="http://disasters.data.gov" class="local-link">disasters.data.gov</a> site, launching today.</p> 
        <p>First previewed at the <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/05/05/brian-forde-sandy-technology/" class="external ext-link" rel="external" onclick="this.target='_blank';">White House Innovation for Disaster Response and Recovery Initiative</a>&nbsp;<a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/2014/08/04/more-1500-people-participate-white-house-innovation-disaster-response-and-recovery-d" class="external ext-link" rel="external" onclick="this.target='_blank';">Demo Day</a>, <a href="http://disasters.data.gov" class="local-link">disasters.data.gov</a> is designed to be a public resource to foster collaboration and the continual improvement of disaster-related open data, free tools, and new ways to empower first responders, survivors, and government officials with the information needed in the wake of a disaster.</p> 
        <p>Today, the Administration is unveiling the first in a series of&nbsp;<a title="Innovator Challenge" href="/disasters/innovator-challenge/"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #0066cc;">Innovator Challenges</span></span></a> that highlight pressing needs from the disaster preparedness community. The inaugural Innovator Challenge focuses on a need identified from firsthand experience of local emergency management, responders, survivors, and Federal departments and agencies. The challenge asks innovators across the nation: “How might we leverage real-time sensors, open data, social media, and other tools to help reduce the number of fatalities from flooding?”</p> 
        <p>&nbsp;</p> 
        <p><em>In addition to this first Innovator Challenge, here are some highlights from </em><a href="http://disasters.data.gov" class="local-link"><em>disasters.data.gov</em></a><em>:</em></p> 
        <ul> 
         <li><a href="https://www.data.gov/disasters/types/" class="local-link"><strong>“Types of Disasters” Landing Pages</strong></a><strong>: </strong>Disasters take many forms. The <a href="http://disasters.data.gov" class="local-link">data.gov</a> portal brings together and categorizes open data sets, apps, and tools to make relevant resources easier to find. Today, the site includes landing pages for <a href="https://www.data.gov/disasters/types/earthquakes" class="local-link">Earthquakes</a>, <a href="https://www.data.gov/disasters/types/floods" class="local-link">Floods</a>, <a href="https://www.data.gov/disasters/types/hurricanes" class="local-link">Hurricanes</a>, <a href="https://www.data.gov/disasters/types/severe-winter-weather" class="local-link">Severe Winter Weather</a>, <a href="https://www.data.gov/disasters/types/tornadoes" class="local-link">Tornadoes</a>, and <a href="https://www.data.gov/disasters/types/wildfires" class="local-link">Wildfires</a>. More categories and content will be added as the website expands.</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>&nbsp;</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li><a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/apps-tools/" class="local-link"><strong>Apps &amp; Tools</strong></a><strong>:</strong> The portal includes an initial set of apps and tools that can be deployed at minimal cost by first responders, emergency managers, volunteer organizations, survivors, and other stakeholders. The apps and tools featured were presented at the White House Innovation for Disaster Response and Recovery&nbsp;<a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/2014/08/04/more-1500-people-participate-white-house-innovation-disaster-response-and-recovery-d" class="external ext-link" rel="external" onclick="this.target='_blank';">Demo Day</a>&nbsp;and include sharing economy platforms, crowdsourcing options, and services to enable communications, contribute to a culture of preparedness, and alert the public.</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>&nbsp;</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li><a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/call-action-open-data/" class="local-link"><strong>Get Involved – Call to Data Stewards</strong></a><strong>:</strong> Datasets relevant to disaster preparedness (including prevention, protection, mitigation, response, and recovery) have traditionally been closed by default to the public. To help empower the community with information that can improve community resilience, the Administration is working with&nbsp;stakeholders&nbsp;to open a series of disaster-related datasets from all levels of government and the private sector. Learn more <a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/call-action-open-data/" class="local-link">here</a>.</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>&nbsp;</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li><a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/join-movement/" class="local-link"><strong>Get Involved – Join the “Innovation for Disasters” Movement</strong></a><strong>:</strong> Whether you visit the portal as a tech entrepreneur, developer, hardware tinkerer, journalist, researcher, government official, first responder, or potential volunteer, there are numerous ways to join the Innovation for Disasters movement and get involved. Learn more <a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/join-movement/" class="local-link">here</a>.</li> 
        </ul> 
        <p>&nbsp;</p> 
        <p>The&nbsp;White House Innovation for Disaster Response and Recovery Initiative&nbsp;was launched by the Administration in response to Hurricane Sandy to find the most effective ways for technology to empower survivors, first responders, and all levels of government with critical information and resources. After over two years of continued dialogue and <a href="https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/sites/default/files/microsites/ostp/white_house_innovation_for_disaster_response_-_2014-july29.pdf" class="external ext-link" rel="external" onclick="this.target='_blank';">major commitments from the public and private sector</a>, the first <a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/2014/11/07/hardware-hacking-disaster-response-red-hook-brooklyn" class="external ext-link" rel="external" onclick="this.target='_blank';">hardware hackathon for disaster preparedness held in support of the Initiative</a>, and numerous workshops, we are thrilled to launch <a href="http://disasters.data.gov" class="local-link">disasters.data.gov</a> as the Initiative’s first major online presence.</p> 
        <p><a href="http://disasters.data.gov" class="local-link">Disasters.data.gov</a> seeks to leverage the creativity and social entrepreneurship of innovators everywhere to help strengthen national preparedness. The Administration will continue working to shape the portal into a useful “go-to” resource that captures a diverse range of contributions in disaster preparedness technology and innovation. We invite you to take the site for a spin, email <a href="mailto:disastertech@ostp.gov?subject=Disasters.data.gov%20Open%20Data">disastertech@ostp.gov</a> with your feedback, and <a href="http://www.data.gov/disasters/get-involved/" class="local-link">get involved</a> to help build a more resilient community.</p>
