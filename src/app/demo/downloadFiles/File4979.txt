                <div class="body"> 
                 <p class="title">Raising the Minimum Wage to $15</p>
                 <div class="program-embed">
                  <p>Raising the minimum wage to $15 an hour statewide was a major priority of Governor Cuomo's 2016 Built to Lead Agenda. The Governor's plan takes the needs of workers and businesses alike into account.</p> 
                  <p>On December 31, 2018, the third wage increase went into effect, enacting the $15 minimum wage for workers in New York City employed by businesses with 11 or more employees.</p>
                  <p>The <a href="https://www.ny.gov/new-york-states-minimum-wage/new-york-states-minimum-wage">wage increases</a> are calibrated by industry and by region in order to provide businesses ample time to adjust. Additional information is available for <a href="https://www.labor.ny.gov/formsdocs/wp/Part146.pdf">tipped</a>, <a href="https://www.labor.ny.gov/formsdocs/wp/Part146.pdf">fast food</a> and <a href="https://www.labor.ny.gov/workerprotection/laborstandards/workprot/minwage.shtm">other workers</a> not covered by the general wage.</p>
