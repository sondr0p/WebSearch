                      <p><strong>New York State’s Minimum Wage&nbsp;</strong></p>
                      <p>As part of the 2016-17 State Budget, Governor Cuomo signed legislation enacting a statewide $15 minimum wage plan that will lift the earnings of more than 2.1 million New Yorkers, in all industries across the state. The Governor’s plan takes the needs of workers and businesses alike into account.</p>
                      <p>&nbsp;</p>
                      <p>As of December 31, 2016, the first in a series of wage increases will go into effect.&nbsp; Rates will differ based on region and industry because the increases are calibrated to provide businesses ample time to adjust.&nbsp;</p>

               <a name="business-responsibilities-under-the-law"></a> 
               <div class="field-item even wysiwyg ">

                 <div class="entity entity-field-collection-item field-collection-item-field-chapters" about="/field-collection/field-chapters/49441" typeof="">

                      Business Responsibilities Under the Law
                     </div>
                    </div>
                   </div>
                   <div class="field field--name-field-chapter-type field--type-list-text field--label-hidden">
                    <div class="field__items">
                     <div class="field__item even">
                      WYSIWYG
                     </div>
                    </div>

                   <div class="field field--name-field-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden">
                    <div class="field__items">
                     <div class="field__item even">
                      <p>The Minimum Wage Act (Article 19 of the New York State Labor Law) requires that all employees in New York State receive <strong>at least $11.10 an hour</strong> beginning December 31, 2018.</p>
                      <p>Minimum wage rates differ based on industry, region and business size. Rates will increase each year until they reach $15.00 per hour.&nbsp;</p>
                      <ul>
                       <li>Fast food industry workers in NYC - $15.00</li>
                       <li>Fast food industry workers outside NYC - $12.75</li>
                       <li>Long Island &amp; Westchester County - $12.00</li>
                       <li>Large NYC employers (of 11 or more) - $15.00</li>
                       <li>Small NYC employers (10 or less) - $13.50</li>
                       <li>Tipped workers&nbsp;- rates vary be region. See <a href="https://labor.ny.gov/formsdocs/wp/Part146.pdf" target="_blank">Wage Order Summary</a>.<a href="https://www.ny.gov/new-york-states-minimum-wage/new-york-states-minimum-wage#business-responsibilities-under-the-law" target="_blank"><br></a></li>
                       <li>Remainder of New York State - $11.10</li>
                      </ul>
                      <p>These rates are in effect from December 31, 2018 through December 30, 2019. <a href="https://labor.ny.gov/workerprotection/laborstandards/workprot/minwage.shtm" target="_blank">View minimum wage rates through 2021</a>.&nbsp;</p>
                      <p><strong>Enforcement of Minimum Wage Laws</strong></p>
                      <p>New York State Department of Labor helps collect underpayments for workers who have not received the minimum wage. Often, funds are collected without resorting to court action.</p>
                      <p>However, an employer that violates the Minimum Wage Law is subject to criminal prosecution and penalties. Action may also be taken in civil court.</p>
                      <p>The Commissioner of Labor may require an employer to pay:</p>
                      <ul>
                       <li>Minimum wage underpayments and liquidated damages, PLUS</li>
                       <li>Interest and civil penalties up to 200% of the unpaid wages</li>
                      </ul>
                      <p><strong>Educational Seminars</strong></p>
                      <p>The Division of Labor Standards offers free educational seminars to employers doing business in New York State.</p>
                      <p>What's in it for you?</p>
                      <ul>
                       <li>Each year Labor Standards responds to thousands of employee complaints of unpaid wages and benefits, illegal employment of children, meal period violations and more.</li>
                       <li>The New York State labor law can be very complicated and it is easy for employers to make a mistake. Many honorable employers are found to owe back wages and penalties that turn out to be quite costly.</li>
                       <li>A Division of Labor Standards Educational Seminar may help an employer avoid the cost and inconvenience of a Labor Standards Investigation. The seminar is designed to teach employers how to avoid being in violation of labor laws.</li>
                       <li>While the labor law covers all areas/types of employment, we can tailor an Educational Seminar to the industry of those attending.</li>
                       <li>We can design a seminar for a specific company, allowing the company to include the seminar in scheduled corporate training sessions for managers or company meetings at a specific location.</li>
                       <li>The Division of Labor Standards will provide Educational Seminar information in brochures, booklets and Powerpoint presentations at no cost to an employer.</li>
                      </ul>
                      <p>&nbsp;</p>
                      <p>For more information on Educational Seminars please call: 518-457-9000.</p>
