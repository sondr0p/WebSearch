           <h1>Plan Your Research Visit</h1> 
           <h3>Do you need to visit us in person?</h3> 
           <ul> 
            <li>We perform limited research for the public. Please read more about our <a href="/publications/general-info-leaflets/71-07-dc-area-records.html">Services for Off-site Researchers</a> and our <a href="/publications/general-info-leaflets/71-05-dc-area-records.html">Research Support Services</a>.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>This reference paper on <a class="pdfImage" href="/files/research/start/getting-started.pdf">Getting Started</a> provides information on the process for requesting records for use in the Research Room and the steps necessary to locate the information needed to prepare a pull slip<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>If you can identify the records precisely enough to order copies, you may <a href="https://eservices.archives.gov/orderonline">order copies of some record types online</a>, or you may <a href="/research/order/">order copies</a> through us or approved vendors.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>If you require extensive research assistance, we offer you the following <a href="/research/hire-help/">lists of researchers who are available for hire</a>.<br> (Note: Researchers for hire are not National Archives employees. <a href="/research/hire-help/index.html">Learn more...</a>)</li> 
           </ul> 
           <h3>Contact us before you visit - write or call ahead</h3> 
           <!-- <img src="/global-images/icons/24x24/snail-mail.gif" alt="mail icon" />&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<img src="/global-images/icons/24x24/phone-red.gif" alt="red phone icon" />&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
<img src="/global-images/icons/24x24/email.gif" alt="email icon" /><br />
<br /> --> 
           <ul> 
            <li><strong><a href="/locations/">Find addresses, phone numbers, hours and directions to our locations nationwide</a></strong>.<br> Our research rooms are open to the public. Each of our locations holds different groups of records. To make the most efficient use of your time, please write, telephone, or <a href="/contact/">e-mail</a> us in advance to make sure that the documents or the microfilm you want to see are at the location you plan to visit.<br> <br> You should first try to determine where the records are by: 
             <ul> 
              <li>Using the <a href="/research/catalog/">National Archives Catalog</a><br> &nbsp;</li> 
              <li>For microfilm available, search the <a href="https://eservices.archives.gov/orderonline/start.swe?SWECmd=GotoView&amp;SWEScreen=GPEA+Microfilm+MIF&amp;SWEView=GPEA+Microfilm+Landing+Page+View+MIF">Microfilm Catalog</a><br> &nbsp;</li> 
              <li>Check the <a href="/research/guide-fed-records/">Guide to Federal Records</a></li> 
             </ul> </li> 
           </ul> 
           <ul> 
            <li><strong>For us to be able to answer your questions properly, you should be able to tell us:</strong> 
             <ul> 
              <li>the Federal and/or presidential connection to your topic;</li> 
              <li>what agencies, offices, or individuals were involved and what time period you are interested in;</li> 
              <li>and what <a href="/research/start/by-format.html">kinds of records</a> (textual, maps, photographs, electronic, etc.) you are looking for.</li> 
             </ul> </li> 
           </ul> 
           <ul> 
            <li><strong>If you expect to use records that may be security classified</strong>,<br> advance notice is necessary so that the classification status of the records can be determined using the <a href="/foia/">Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)</a>.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li><strong>When sending a letter or <a href="/contact/">e-mail</a></strong>,<br> include <strong> your postal address and telephone number </strong> so that we can contact you in case more information is needed. Please allow time for us to respond.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li><a class="moreLink" href="/research/start/research-visit-faqs.html">Learn more about how to make your research visit to NARA more successful</a></li> 
           </ul> 
           <h2>When You Arrive</h2> 
           <ul> 
            <li>Make your initial visit to any National Archives location, even those with extended hours 
             <ul> 
              <li>during weekday business hours</li> 
              <li>as early in the day as possible</li> 
             </ul> </li> 
           </ul> 
           <ul> 
            <li>Archivists whose help is needed to begin research usually are not available during evenings or weekends. A late afternoon visit may not allow enough time for records to be identified and retrieved from the stack areas for use on that day.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>When you come to one of our locations to look at records, you will usually be issued a <a href="/research/start/researcher-card.html">researcher identification card</a>.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>During your research visit, keep good notes as to records consulted so that you can differentiate between multiple groups of records searched, and your steps can be retraced if necessary.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>Anyone can use the National Archives. You do not need to be an American citizen or to present credentials or a letter of recommendation. Read about <a href="/research/start/nara-regulations.html">Regulations for using the National Archives</a>.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>You need to be <a href="/research/start/access-by-minors.html">14 years old to do research</a> at the National Archives, unless you receive permission from the Research Center Branch Chief and are accompanied by a parent or guardian.<br> &nbsp;</li> 
            <li>Read about <a href="/research/notices/new-security-procedures.html">Security Procedures</a> at our facilities.<br> &nbsp;</li>
