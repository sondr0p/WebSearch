       <p>If you apply for Social Security or Supplemental Security Income benefits, a new law went into effect March 1, 2013, requiring that you receive your payments electronically. If you did not sign up for electronic payments when you applied for benefits, we strongly urge you to do it now. If you still receive checks, the U.S. Department of the Treasury will contact you about complying with the requirement. For more information regarding switching to an electronic payment, visit Treasury’s <a href="http://godirect.org/">Go Direct website</a> or call the helpline at <strong>1-800-333-1795</strong>.</p> 
       <p>It's <a href="safe.htm" class="definition">safe</a>, <a href="simple.htm" class="definition">quick</a> and <a href="convenient.htm" class="definition">convenient</a>.</p> 
       <p>Treasury can grant exceptions in rare circumstances. For more information or to request a waiver, call Treasury at 855-290-1545.&nbsp;You may also print and fill out a waiver form and return it to the address on the form.&nbsp;<a href="EFT Waiver Form.pdf">Waiver Form</a></p> 
       <p>If you already are receiving benefits, you can create a <a href="https://secure.ssa.gov/RIL/"><span class="my">my </span> <span class="lb">Social Security </span></a>account and start or change Direct Deposit online. You also can sign up at your bank, credit union, or savings and loan.</p> 
       <p>Or call Social Security at <strong>1-800-772-1213</strong> (TTY <strong>1-800-325-0778</strong>).</p>
