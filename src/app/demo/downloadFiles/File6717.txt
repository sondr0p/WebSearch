               <article class="node node-index-list node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky odd clearfix" about="/elected-officials" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document"> 
                <span property="dc:title" content="Elected Officials" class="rdf-meta element-hidden"></span> 
                <div> 
                 <span class="print-link"></span>
                 <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"> 
                  <div class="field-items"> 
                   <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                    <h4><a href="http://gov.ga.gov">Governor —&nbsp;Brian P. Kemp</a></h4> 
                    <p><img alt="Governor Kemp" src="/sites/georgia/files/kemp_circle.png" style="width: 143px; height: 120px; float: left;">The 83<sup>rd</sup>&nbsp;Governor of Georgia, Brian P. Kemp&nbsp;took office in January 2019.</p> 
                    <p>Kemp’s political career began when he served as a state senator from 2003-07. &nbsp;He served as Georgia Secretary of State from 2010-18.&nbsp;</p> 
                    <p>He is a business owner, entrepreneur, and investor.&nbsp;</p> 
                    <hr>
                    <div class="field-item odd"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://ltgov.georgia.gov">Lieutenant Governor —&nbsp;Geoff Duncan</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Lieutenant Governor Duncan" src="/sites/georgia/files/duncan_circle.png" style="width: 143px; height: 120px; float: left;">Geoff Duncan took office in January 2019.</p> 
                      <p>Duncan served in the Georgia House of Representatives from 2013&nbsp;until his election as Lieutenant Governor in the 2018 general election.&nbsp;</p> 
                      <p>He is a business owner&nbsp;and former minor league baseball pitcher.</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://www.house.ga.gov/Representatives/en-US/HouseMembersList.aspx">Georgia House of Representatives</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="House seal" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/house.png" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;">The House of Representatives consists of 180 members from regions around the state. Each January the House convenes to set an annual operating budget, amend the prior year's budget, and craft laws to address issues ranging from education reform to transportation investment.</p> 
                      <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item odd"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://www.senate.ga.gov/senators/en-US/SenateMembersList.aspx">Georgia Senate</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Senate Seal" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/senate.png" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;">Each January, 56 senators meet to set an annual operating budget, amend the current year's budget, and craft laws that range from promoting business to keeping health care spending in check.</p> 
                      <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://law.ga.gov">Attorney General — Chris Carr</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Attorney General Carr" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/ChrisCarr-circle.jpg" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;"></p> 
                      <p>Christopher M. Carr was sworn into office as Georgia's 54<sup>th</sup> Attorney General on November 1, 2016. He was elected to a new term in the&nbsp;2018 general election. Carr believes there is no more solemn responsibility than to protect and defend the Constitution of the United States, the Constitution and laws of the State of Georgia and the interests of the people of the State of Georgia.</p> 
                      <p>Carr previously served as Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Economic Development under Governor Nathan Deal and as Chief of Staff for U.S. Senator Johnny Isakson.</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item odd"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://sos.georgia.gov/">Secretary of State —&nbsp;Brad Raffensperger</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Secretary of State Raffensperger" src="/sites/georgia/files/raffensperger_circle.png" style="width: 143px; height: 120px; float: left;">Brad Raffensperger took office in January 2019.</p> 
                      <p>Raffensperger&nbsp;served in the Georgia House of Representatives from 2015&nbsp;until his election as Secretary of State&nbsp;in the 2018 general election.&nbsp;</p> 
                      <p>He is an engineer and business owner.</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://www.doe.k12.ga.us/">State School Superintendent — Richard Woods</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Superintendent Woods" src="/sites/georgia/files/photo_gallery/RichardWoodsCircle.jpg" style="width: 143px; height: 120px; float: left;">Richard Woods took office in January 2015 and was re-elected in the 2018 general election.</p> 
                      <p>He works on behalf of the 1.7 million K-12 students in Georgia’s public schools, championing child-focused, classroom-centered policies.</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item odd"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://agr.georgia.gov">Commissioner of Agriculture — Gary W. Black</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner Black" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/agriculture-black.png" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;">After serving more than 2 decades as the president of the Georgia Agribusiness Council, Commissioner Gary W. Black accepted office in January 2011. He has been re-elected twice since.</p> 
                      <p>He champions food safety, science-based environmental stewardship, and, through <a href="http://georgiagrown.com">Georgia Grown</a>, the local agriculture movement.</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://www.gainsurance.org/">Commissioner of Insurance —&nbsp;Jim Beck</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner Beck" src="/sites/georgia/files/beck_circle.png" style="width: 143px; height: 120px; float: left;">Jim Beck took office in January 2019.</p> 
                      <p>Beck graduated from the University of West Georgia with a bachelor's degree&nbsp;in marketing. His background includes working in an independent insurance agency, serving for 12&nbsp;years in the area of public affairs for a Fortune 150 property and casualty insurance company, and leading an operation that provides propertyinsurance to those who are locked out of the open market.</p> 
                      <p>He previously served as Deputy Insurance Commissioner as well as Chief of Staff for&nbsp;former Commissioner Ralph Hudgens.</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item odd"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://www.dol.state.ga.us/">Commissioner of Labor — Mark Butler</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner Butler" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/labor-butler.png" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;">A former legislator in the State House of Representatives, Commissioner Mark Butler took office in January 2011 and has been re-elected twice.</p> 
                      <p>Aiming for a full-hire economy, he focuses on helping job seekers apply for and receive unemployment benefits and forges partnership with private industry to connect those job seekers with employers.</p> 
                      <hr>
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="field-item even"> 
                     <h4 class="related-link"><a href="http://www.psc.state.ga.us">Public Service Commission</a></h4> 
                     <div class="related-description"> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner Shaw" src="/sites/georgia/files/jason_shaw_circle.jpg" style="width: 143px; height: 120px; float: left;">A former state representative, Commissioner <strong>Jason Shaw&nbsp;</strong>took office in January 2019. He represents PSC District 1, the southern portion of the state that stretches from Macon to Savannah.</p> 
                      <p>Read <a href="http://www.psc.state.ga.us/content.aspx?c=/commissioners//jason-shaw/&amp;tab=2">Commissioner Shaw's views on utilities and energy innovation</a>.</p> 
                      <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner Echols" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/echols.png" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;">A veteran of non-profit work who focuses on consumer protection and accountability, Commissioner <strong>Tim Echols</strong> took office in January 2011. He represents PSC District 2, which covers the eastern central portion of the state running from Gwinnett to Screven counties.</p> 
                      <p>He works to promote clean energy initiatives and speaks the importance of safe disposal of nuclear waste.</p> 
                      <p>Read <a href="http://www.psc.state.ga.us/pscinfo/bios/echols.asp">Commissioner Echols' views on utilities and energy innovation</a>.</p> 
                      <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner Eaton" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/eaton.png" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;">Commissioner&nbsp;<strong>Chuck Eaton </strong>took office in January 2007. He represents PSC District 3, the metro Atlanta area.</p> 
                      <p>To gain a better understanding of the judicial issues he faces at the commission, Commissioner Eaton began attending classes at Georgia State Law School in 2009 and studies nights as he works toward his degree.</p> 
                      <p>Read <a href="http://www.psc.state.ga.us/pscinfo/bios/eaton.asp">Commissioner Eaton's views on utilities and energy innovation</a>.</p> 
                      <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner McDonald" src="/sites/georgia/files/elected_officials/mcdonalds.png" style="width: 140px; height: 120px; float: left;">A former state representative and re-elected member of the commission, Commission Chairman&nbsp;<strong>Lauren "Bubba" McDonald, Jr.</strong> represents PSC District 4, which ranges across the north Georgia mountains to the South Carolina border.</p> 
                      <p>Read <a href="http://www.psc.state.ga.us/pscinfo/bios/mcdonald.asp">Commissioner McDonald's views on utilities and energy innovation</a>.</p> 
                      <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                      <p><img alt="Commissioner Pridemore" src="/sites/georgia/files/pridemore_circle.jpg" style="width: 143px; height: 120px; float: left;">Commissioner&nbsp;<strong>Tricia Pridemore</strong> was appointed by Governor Nathan Deal to fill a vacancy on the Commission in 2018. She was elected to a new term in the 2018 general election. She represents PSC District 5, the west central portion of the state that includes Cobb, Douglas, and Carroll counties.</p> 
                      <p>She is a businesswoman with a background in technology, consulting and workforce development.&nbsp;</p> 
                      <p>Read <a href="http://www.psc.state.ga.us/content.aspx?c=/commissioners/tricia-pridemore/">Commissioner Pridemore's views on utilities and energy innovation</a>.</p> 
                     </div>
