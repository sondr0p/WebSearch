                    <p dir="ltr">The 2019 legislative year is in full swing, and members of the 155<sup>th</sup> Georgia General Assembly are at work.</p> 
                    <h2 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">2019 Legislative Timeline</span></h2> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Here are the past and upcoming major dates associated with this year’s legislation:</span></p> 
                    <ul style="list-style-type:disc;">
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">January 9: </span><strong>Opening Day</strong><br>This year, the first day of the session coincided with inauguration day and the swearing-in of a new <a href="https://georgia.gov/elected-officials">executive branch</a> as well as a new General Assembly.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">March 7: </span><strong>Crossover Day</strong><br>The 28<sup>th</sup> day of the session is the last day one chamber can pass legislation to the other. Bills that fail to cross over are usually dead, at least for now.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">April 2: </span><strong>Sine Die</strong><br>By law, the General Assembly meets for only 40 days each year; this is day 40. Any bill that has not passed both the Senate and the House of Representatives will not become law, at least not this year. (However, since 2019 is year one of a two-year legislative cycle, bills may resurface for consideration in 2020.)</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">May 12: </span><strong>Signing deadline</strong><br>By law, the governor has 40 days after the end of the session to consider whether to sign or veto this year’s passed bills. Any passed bills left untouched by the governor become law.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">July 1: </span><strong>Bills effective</strong><br>All bills passed during the 2019 session become law on this date, unless they were specifically written to take effect on a different date.</p> </li> 
                    </ul>
                    <h2 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Follow the Bouncing Bills</span></h2> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">To find the status of a particular bill, visit the </span><a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov">General Assembly’s website</a> and use the legislation search box at the top left of the page. You can also use the <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/Search.aspx">advanced search function</a> to find bills by keyword or sponsor.</p> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">On each bill’s page, there is a summary that lists all the steps it has taken so far in the legislative process: when it was first introduced, what committee reviewed it, whether it has been voted on in either chamber, etc.</span></p> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Importantly, each page also includes a copy of the exact language of the bill in its current form. In the case of bills that would modify existing law, the text is marked so it is easy to tell what’s changing:</span></p> 
                    <p dir="ltr" style="margin-left:36pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">A bill’s </span><s>original language is marked with strikethrough text and</s> <u>new language is underlined</u>.</p> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Each bill’s page also includes general information, such as who sponsored it.</span></p> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Once a bill has won approval in both chambers it goes to the Governor, who may sign it into law or veto. As the Governor makes these decisions, they are </span><a href="https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases">announced on his website</a>.</p> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">While a few bills are signed promptly after passage, the Governor has 40 days from the end of the legislative session to sign or veto; if no action is taken, the bill becomes law at the end of this time. For many high-profile bills, the Governor may wait until very near the deadline before announcing his decision. (Expect a flurry of activity in early May this year.)</span></p> 
                    <h3 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Summary of Process</span></h3> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">The </span><a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/default.aspx">complete legislative process</a> is outlined on the Georgia General Assembly’s website. Check out our <a href="https://georgia.gov/blog/2016-06-21/life-law-infographic">Life of a Law Infographic</a> for a visual summary of this process.</p> 
                    <h2 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Notable Bills</span></h2> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Here are some notable bills from this session and their current status (as of March 6):</span></p> 
                    <h4 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Awaiting Passage</span></h4> 
                    <ul style="list-style-type:disc;">
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Abortion</span></strong>: The House is considering legislation that would limit or outlaw abortion: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/HB/481">HB 481</a> and <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/HB/546">HB 546</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c"><strong>Airport Control</strong></span>: The Senate is considering legislation that would shift control of Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport from the city to the state: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/SB/131">SB 131</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c"><strong>Horse Race Betting</strong></span>: The Senate is considering legislation that would allow horse racing, with betting allowed on the outcomes: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/SR/84">SB 84</a> and <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/SB/45">SB 45</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c"><strong>Hospital Need</strong></span>: The House and Senate are each considering legislation that would simplify the application process for creating new health care facilities: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/SB/74">SB 74</a> and <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/HB/198">HB 198</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Rural Airport Funding</span></strong>: The House is considering legislation that would increase funding to rural airports, via an additional jet fuel tax: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/HB/447">HB 447</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Tax on Digital Goods/Services</span></strong>: The House is considering legislation that would tax digital goods and services: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/HB/428">HB 428</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Transportation</span></strong>: The House is considering legislation to create a new statewide transit/transportation agency, to focus primarily on needs in rural Georgia: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/HB/511">HB 511</a>.</p> </li> 
                    </ul>
                    <h4 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Partially Passed</span></h4> 
                    <ul style="list-style-type:disc;">
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Budget</span></strong>: The House passed the fiscal year 2020 budget, a $27.5 billion spending plan. Highlights include raises for teachers and state employees, funding for new voting machines, additional education funding, and additional Medicaid funding: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/HB/31">HB 31</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c"><strong>Dyslexia Testing</strong></span>: The Senate passed legislation that would provide support for dyslexia testing of students in pre-kindergarten through second grade: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/SB/48">SB 48</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Elections</span></strong>: The House passed legislation affecting the type of voting machines to be used, “exact match” voter registration, and absentee ballot applications: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/HB/316">HB 316</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c"><strong>Hemp Farming</strong></span>: The House passed legislation that would allow hemp farming: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/display/20192020/HB/213">HB 213</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c"><strong>Medicare/Medicaid</strong></span>: The Senate passed one of Governor Kemp’s signature proposals, the “Patients First Act,” which would affect how the state delivers Medicare and Medicaid services: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/SB/106">SB 106</a>.</p> </li> 
                    </ul>
                    <h4 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Passed and Signed by Governor</span></h4> 
                    <ul style="list-style-type:disc;">
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">School Bus Safety</span></strong>: The <a href="https://gov.georgia.gov/press-releases/2019-02-18/gov-kemp-signs-school-bus-safety-law">first bill signed by newly elected Governor Brian P. Kemp</a> amends existing law to require drivers to stop for a stopped school bus, unless “separated by a grass median, unpaved area, or physical barrier”: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/SB/25">Act 1</a>.</p> </li> 
                     <li dir="ltr"> <p dir="ltr"><strong><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">City of Skidaway Island</span></strong>: This legislation puts forth a referendum on the incorporation of the city of Skidaway Island in Chatham County: <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/legislation/en-US/Display/20192020/HB/29">Act 2</a>.</p> </li> 
                    </ul>
                    <h2 dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">Let Your Voice Be Heard</span></h2> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">It’s always good to let your elected officials — the </span><a href="https://georgia.gov/blog/2017-05-02/who-represents-you-part-1-state-legislators">Senator and Representative for the area you live</a> — know how you feel about a bill. Additionally, contact the sponsor(s) of a particular bill as well as members of the committee which is considering it.</p> 
                    <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-eadf04cd-7fff-e3cf-4661-ea27b1a1e98c">You can find a specific bill’s sponsors, with links to their contact information, on the information page for a particular bill. (Again, to find any bill, go to </span><a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov">legis.ga.gov</a> and use the search box at the top left of the page. There is also <a href="http://www.legis.ga.gov/Legislation/en-US/Search.aspx">an advanced search option</a> that allows you to find bills by keyword or sponsor.)</p> 
                   </div>
