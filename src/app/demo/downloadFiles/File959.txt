       <h2 id="item-213996">Infographic: Get Help with Living Expenses</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p> Federal government benefit programs can help people with a low income cover basic expenses like food, housing, and healthcare. </p> 
      <div class="fea-cntntmddl-img"> 
       <img src="//app-usa-modeast-prod-a01239f.s3.amazonaws.com/Government_Benefits_Final_2019.png" alt="Infographic explaining government benefits for people with a low income."> 
      </div> 
      <p class="lnk-icon"> <img src="/sites/all/themes/usa/images/Icon_New_Screen.png" width="30" height="26" alt="Infographic explaining government benefits for people with a low income."> <a href="https://app-usa-modeast-prod-a01239f.s3.amazonaws.com/Government_Benefits_Final_2019_Hires.png" aria-labelledby="item-213996" onmousedown="_sendEvent('Outbound Downloads','pdf','https://app-usa-modeast-prod-a01239f.s3.amazonaws.com/Government_Benefits_Final_2019_Hires.png',0);"> View a larger version of the infographic. </a> </p> 
      <div class="clearfix"> 
       <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="infograph-b-213996"> 
        <div class="usa-accordion-bordered"> 
         <ul> 
          <li class="transcript_img"> <button class="usa-accordion-button" data-toggledtext="Hide Description of Infographic" data-initialtext="Show Description of Infographic" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="infograph-b-213996" aria-labelledby="item-213996">Show Description of Infographic </button> 
           <div id="infograph-b-213996" class="usa-accordion-content">
            <p></p>
            <p>If you have a low income and need help with basic living expenses, you may qualify for government benefits to help cover food, housing, medical, and other costs.</p>
            <h3>Major Government Benefits Programs</h3>
            <p>The federal government creates and gives money to states to run major assistance programs. Your state helps pay for some of these and may offer others too.</p>
            <h4>Food</h4>
            <p><strong>Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP, or food stamps)</strong> provides eligible people with benefits cards, used like debit cards, to buy food at authorized grocery stores and farmers markets.</p>
            <p>Other food aid programs offer:</p>
            <ul>
             <li><p>Healthy foods to pregnant women, new moms, and kids under age 5</p></li>
             <li><p>Free or low-cost meals or milk to kids at schools and daycare centers</p></li>
             <li><p>Monthly food packages and farmers market coupons to seniors</p></li>
            </ul>
            <h4>Healthcare</h4>
            <p><strong>Medicaid </strong>provides free or low-cost health benefits to adults, kids, pregnant women, seniors, and people with disabilities.</p>
            <p><strong>Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP)</strong> offers free or low-cost medical and dental care to uninsured kids up to age 19 whose family income is above Medicaid’s limit but below their state’s CHIP limit.</p>
            <h4>Housing</h4>
            <p><strong>Subsidized Housing, Housing Vouchers, and Public Housing programs</strong> help low-income families, seniors, and people with disabilities get into affordable private or government-owned rental housing.</p>
            <p><strong>Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)</strong> helps low-income households pay heating and cooling bills and offers low-cost home improvements to reduce those costs.&nbsp;</p>
            <h4>Financial Assistance</h4>
            <p><strong>Welfare or Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)</strong> provides cash for a limited time to low-income families working toward self-sufficiency. TANF may also offer non-cash benefits such as child care and job training.</p>
            <p><strong>Supplemental Security Income (SSI)</strong> provides cash to low-income seniors and low-income adults and kids with disabilities.</p>
            <h3>Eligibility and Application Requirements</h3>
            <ul>
             <li><p>All programs have income limits.</p></li>
             <li><p>Most programs require you to be a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen.</p></li>
             <li><p>Many programs have other state requirements like a family’s size and financial resources.</p></li>
            </ul>
            <p>Learn more about who’s eligible and how to apply for these programs at Benefits.gov or your state’s social, human, or health services office.</p>
            <h3>Grants and Loans Are Not Benefits</h3>
            <p><strong>Don’t believe ads</strong> for “free government grants” to start a business or pay personal expenses.<strong> The federal government does not give grants to individuals.</strong> It awards grants to states, universities, and other organizations.</p>
            <p>You may be able to get a government loan to help with school, housing, and business costs, but <strong>you must repay it,</strong> typically with interest.</p>
            <p>Learn more at USA.gov/benefits-grants-loans.</p>
            <p>Brought to you by USAGov</p>
            <p></p>
           </div> </li> 
         </ul> 
        </div> 
       </article> 
      </div> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-211654"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-211654">Benefits and Financial Assistance from the Government</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>If you're looking for immediate or emergency help, your <a href="https://www.usa.gov/states-and-territories">state's human service or social service agency</a> might be able to provide financial assistance or refer you to local community organizations.</p>
      <h3>Search for Financial Assistance from the Government</h3>
      <p>Benefits.gov is a free website that can help you determine which types of government assistance you might qualify for and how and where to apply.</p>
      <ul>
       <li><a href="http://www.benefits.gov/benefits/benefit-finder#benefits&amp;qc=cat_1">Using the Benefit Finder</a>, answer questions about yourself and your needs to see if you’re eligible for programs to help you pay for food, health insurance, or other necessities.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>Check back with <a href="http://www.benefits.gov/">Benefits.gov</a> in the future to see if you’re eligible for additional benefits after major life events and as new benefit programs become available.</p>
      <h3>Search for Benefits and Programs for People with Disabilities</h3>
      <p>Find government and local disability programs including <a href="https://www.usa.gov/disability-programs">housing, jobs, education, and health care</a>.</p>
      <p>If you receive Social Security disability benefits (SSDI), review these <a href="https://www.usa.gov/about-social-security">top questions about Social Security</a>.</p>
      <h3>Search for Benefits for Military Veterans and Their Families</h3>
      <p><a href="https://www.usa.gov/veterans">Learn about veterans benefits</a> such as burial, education, health, and housing.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-35882"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-35882">Apply for Unemployment Benefits</h2> 
      </header> 
      <h3>What help is available?</h3>
      <p>There are a variety of benefit and aid programs to help you if you lose your job.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.careeronestop.org/">CareerOneStop.org</a> is a good place to start. It can help with unemployment insurance benefits, job training, and finding a job.</p>
      <h3>Unemployment Insurance</h3>
      <h4>Am I eligible?</h4>
      <p>Unemployment insurance programs pay you money if you lose your job through no fault of your own. You must meet your state's eligibility requirements.</p>
      <h4>How do I apply?</h4>
      <p>Each state runs its own program. <a href="https://www.careeronestop.org/LocalHelp/UnemploymentBenefits/unemployment-benefits.aspx">Select your state from this map</a> to find out how to apply. You may be able to file online, by phone, or in person.&nbsp;</p>
      <h4>Is there anything else I need to know?</h4>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Some states provide extended benefits when there's high unemployment. Extended unemployment insurance benefits last for 13 weeks. You can apply for extended benefits only once you've run out of regular benefits. Check with your state; not everyone qualifies.</p></li>
       <li><p>You must <a href="https://www.irs.gov/taxtopics/tc418">report unemployment benefits as income</a> on your tax return.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Other Types of Benefits and Programs for the Unemployed</h3>
      <h4>Educational Help</h4>
      <p>Federal agencies offer many <a href="https://www.careeronestop.org/LocalHelp/EmploymentAndTraining/employment-and-training.aspx">unemployment education and training programs</a>. They are generally free or low cost to the unemployed.</p>
      <h4>Self-Employment Help</h4>
      <p><a href="http://workforcesecurity.doleta.gov/unemploy/self.asp">Self-employment assistance programs</a> help unemployed workers start their own small businesses. Delaware, Mississippi, New Hampshire, New York, and Oregon offer this program.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-35787"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-35787">Food Stamps (SNAP Food Benefits)</h2> 
      </header> 
      <h3>What help is available through food stamps (SNAP food benefits)?</h3>
      <p>If you have a low income, the <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/supplemental-nutrition-assistance-program-snap">Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)</a>, formerly known as "food stamps," is a federal nutrition program that can help you stretch your food budget.</p>
      <h3>Am I eligible for SNAP?</h3>
      <p>To determine if you are eligible, you must meet certain <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/eligibility">requirements including resource and income limits</a>.&nbsp;</p>
      <h3>How do I apply for SNAP?</h3>
      <p>Find the <a href="http://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/apply">online application for your state</a>, local office addresses, and phone numbers. You may &nbsp;also apply in person at a state or local office.</p>
      <h3>Is there anything else I need to know about SNAP?</h3>
      <ul>
       <li><p>You get benefits each month on a plastic electronic benefits transfer (EBT) card, which you use like a credit or debit card to purchase eligible food items at your local authorized SNAP grocery stores&nbsp;and some farmers markets.</p></li>
       <li><p>Some states have <a href="http://www.fns.usda.gov/ebt/state-ebt-websites">websites set up for managing your benefits</a>. If your state doesn't have an EBT management website, you can contact your <a href="http://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/state-informationhotline-numbers">state SNAP office</a> to check your benefits.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>How do I file a complaint about SNAP?</h3>
      <p>If you need to file a complaint concerning your food stamps or attempt to get food stamps, the following resources can help:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>If you have <a href="http://asktheexpert.custhelp.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/1595">questions about your benefit amount</a>, contact your <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/state-informationhotline-numbers">local field office</a> to request a hearing.</p></li>
       <li><p>File a <a href="https://www.usda.gov/oig/hotline.htm">complaint online or by phone about a SNAP retailer</a>. You will need to give the name and the location of the store. You may remain anonymous if you choose.</p></li>
       <li><p>If you suspect fraud or abuse of the SNAP program, contact your <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/integrity/fraud-reporting-by-state">state's SNAP fraud hotline or website</a>.</p></li>
       <li><p><a href="https://www.ascr.usda.gov/node/119">Learn how to file a complaint</a> if you believe you have experienced discrimination in the SNAP program because of age, sex, color, race, disability, religious creed, national origin, limited English proficiency, or political beliefs.</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>SNAP Information For Retailers</h3>
      <p>Learn how to work with SNAP if you <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/retailer">are a retailer</a> or if you <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/farmer-producer">operate a farmers market</a>.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-36602"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-36602">Welfare or Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p><a href="https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ofa/programs/tanf/about">Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)</a>, also known as welfare, is designed to help families recover from temporary difficulties and move forward.</p>
      <h3>What help is available through TANF?</h3>
      <p>Recipients may qualify for help with food, housing, home energy, child care, job training, and more.</p>
      <p>Each state TANF program is operated differently and <a href="https://aspe.hhs.gov/dataset/tanf-program-names">has a different name</a>.</p>
      <p>Some <a href="https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ofa/programs/tribal/tribal-tanf">tribal groups</a> operate their own TANF programs.</p>
      <h3>Am I eligible for TANF? &nbsp;</h3>
      <ul>
       <li>Each state or tribal territory decides the specific eligibility criteria for financial assistance or other benefits and services.</li>
       <li>You must be a resident of the state where you are applying.</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>How do I apply for TANF?</h3>
      <ul>
       <li>To sign-up for temporary benefits, you can apply at your local or county social services agency. <a href="https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ofa/help">Call your state TANF office</a> for your local contact information.</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>How do I report TANF benefit fraud?</h3>
      <p>If you suspect possible welfare fraud, contact your local TANF office or contact the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) <a href="https://www.usa.gov/federal-agencies/hhs-tips-fraud-hotline">Inspector General’s Fraud Hotline</a>.</p>
      <h3>What else do I need to know about TANF?</h3>
      <p>If you receive TANF, you may be eligible to receive <a href="https://www.usa.gov/benefits#item-211654">other government benefits</a>.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-35462"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-35462">Medicaid and Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP)</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>Medicaid is a&nbsp;federal and state health insurance program for people with a low income.</p>
      <p>The Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP) offers health coverage to children in families with incomes too high to qualify for Medicaid, but who can’t afford private coverage. Medicaid and CHIP program <a href="https://www.healthcare.gov/medicaid-chip-program-names/">names are different in each state</a>.</p>
      <h3>Medicaid</h3>
      <h4>What help is available?</h4>
      <p><a href="https://www.medicaid.gov/">Medicaid</a> provides free or low-cost medical benefits to eligible low-income adults, children, pregnant women, seniors, and people with disabilities.</p>
      <h4>Am I eligible?</h4>
      <p>Check with your state’s Medicaid office to see if you or your family members are <a href="https://www.healthcare.gov/medicaid-chip/">eligible for benefits</a>. In general, it depends on at least one or a combination of</p>
      <ul>
       <li>Age</li>
       <li>Income level</li>
       <li>Number of people in your family</li>
       <li>If you are pregnant or have a disability</li>
      </ul>
      <h4>How do I apply?</h4>
      <p>There are two ways to apply for Medicaid:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><a href="https://www.medicaid.gov/state-overviews/index.html">Contact your state Medicaid agency</a>. You must be a resident of the state where you are applying for benefits.</li>
       <li><a href="https://www.healthcare.gov/create-account">Fill out an application through the Health Insurance Marketplace</a>.</li>
      </ul>
      <h4>How do I complain?</h4>
      <p>If you have a problem with Medicaid or CHIP services, you can file a complaint. These programs are managed by each state rather than the federal government. Contact your <a href="https://www.medicaid.gov/state-overviews/index.html">state's Medicaid program</a> to report your issue.</p>
      <h3>What additional help is available for children?</h3>
      <p>If your income is too high for Medicaid, your child may still qualify for the <a href="https://www.insurekidsnow.gov/index.html">Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP)</a>. It covers medical and <a href="https://www.insurekidsnow.gov/coverage/find-a-dentist/index.html">dental care</a> for uninsured children and teens up to age 19.</p>
      <h4>Is my child eligible for CHIP?</h4>
      <p><a href="http://www.insurekidsnow.gov/coverage/questions/index.html#childEligible">Rules to qualify are different in every state</a>. In most cases, they depend on income.</p>
      <h4>How do I apply?</h4>
      <p>You have two ways to apply for CHIP:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><a href="https://www.insurekidsnow.gov/coverage/index.html">Find a program by state</a> or call 1-877-KIDS-NOW (<a href="tel:18775437669">1-877-543-7669</a>).</li>
       <li>Fill out an application through the <a href="https://www.healthcare.gov/medicaid-chip/getting-medicaid-chip/">Health Insurance Marketplace</a>.</li>
      </ul>
      <h4>Is there anything else I need to know?</h4>
      <ul>
       <li>You can apply for and enroll in Medicaid or CHIP anytime during the year.</li>
       <li><a href="https://www.healthcare.gov/medicaid-chip/using-medicaid-or-chip-coverage/index.html">Learn how to use your Medicaid or CHIP coverage</a>.</li>
      </ul>
      <ul>
       <li>The <a href="https://www.medicaid.gov/basic-health-program/index.html">Basic Health Program (BHP)</a> offers affordable, continued coverage for people whose income shifts above and below Medicaid or CHIP levels.</li>
       <li><a href="https://www.usa.gov/health-insurance">Get information on other common types of health insurance</a>, such as Medicare, and find help paying for medical bills.</li>
      </ul>
