      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-214175">Free Money from the Government </h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>The federal government does not offer grants or “free money” to individuals to&nbsp;start a business&nbsp;or cover personal expenses, contrary to what you might see online or in the media. Websites or other publications claiming to offer "free money from the government"&nbsp;are often scams. <a href="https://www.ftccomplaintassistant.gov/#crnt&amp;panel1-1">Report them</a> to the Federal Trade Commission.&nbsp;</p>
      <p>The government&nbsp; does offer&nbsp;<a href="https://www.usa.gov/benefits" target="_blank">federal benefit programs designed to help individuals and families in need become self-sufficient</a>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<a href="https://www.usa.gov/help-with-bills" target="_blank">lower their expenses</a>.&nbsp;</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-36807"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-36807">Government Grants </h2> 
      </header> 
      <h3>What is a Grant from the Government?</h3>
      <p>A grant is one of the ways the government funds ideas and projects to provide public services and stimulate the economy. Grants support critical recovery initiatives, innovative research, and many other programs listed in the <a href="https://www.cfda.gov/">Catalog of Federal Domestic Assistance</a> (CFDA). <a href="https://www.grants.gov/web/grants/learn-grants/grants-101.html">Learn more about grants and types of grants</a>.</p>
      <h3>Who May Receive a Grant?</h3>
      <p>The federal government typically awards grants to state and local governments, universities, researchers, law enforcement, organizations, and institutions planning major projects that will benefit specific parts of the population or the community as a whole.&nbsp;</p>
      <p>Contrary to what you might see online or in the media, the federal government does not offer grants or “free money” to individuals to <a href="https://www.usa.gov/funding-options">start a business</a>, or cover personal expenses. However, the government offers <a href="https://www.usa.gov/benefits">federal benefit programs designed to help individuals and families in need become self-sufficient</a> or <a href="https://www.usa.gov/help-with-bills">lower their expenses</a>.</p>
      <h3>Search and Apply for Government Grants</h3>
      <p>Use the federal government’s free, official website, <a href="https://www.grants.gov/web/grants/home.html">Grants.gov</a> rather than commercial sites that may charge a fee for grant information or application forms. Grants.gov centralizes information from more than 1,000 government grant programs to help states and organizations find and apply for grants.</p>
      <ul>
       <li><strong>Eligibility -</strong> <a href="https://www.grants.gov/web/grants/learn-grants/grant-eligibility.html">Most of the funding opportunities are for organizations, not individuals</a>.</li>
       <li><strong>How to apply</strong>&nbsp;-&nbsp;<a href="https://www.grants.gov/web/grants/applicants/applicant-training.html">Get tips and tools to help you with the registration and application process</a>. And learn how to <a href="http://writingcenter.unc.edu/handouts/grant-proposals-or-give-me-the-money/">write a successful grant proposal</a>.</li>
       <li><strong>Email notifications</strong>&nbsp;- Sign up to <a href="http://www.grants.gov/web/grants/manage-subscriptions.html">be notified by email about new grant opportunities</a>.</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>"Free" Grants and Grant Scams</h3>
      <p>If you received information stating you qualify for a "free grant," it is probably a scam. <a href="http://www.consumer.ftc.gov/articles/0113-government-grant-scams">Get information from the Federal Trade Commission (FTC)</a> to help you learn to recognize and avoid grant scams. If you have been a victim of a grant scam, you can <a href="https://www.ftccomplaintassistant.gov/#crnt&amp;panel1-1">file a complaint with the FTC</a>.&nbsp;</p>
      <p>Contact your <a href="https://www.usa.gov/state-consumer">state consumer protection office</a> if you have purchased a book or paid a fee to get grant information and are not satisfied.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-37017"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-37017">Government Loans</h2> 
      </header> 
      <h3>What is a Loan from the Government?</h3>
      <p>Government loans serve a specific purpose such as paying for education, helping with housing or business needs, or responding to an emergency or crisis.</p>
      <p>Loans are different than grants because recipients are required to repay loans, often with interest.</p>
      <h4>Examples of Government Loans</h4>
      <p>The federal government offers several types of loans, including:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><a href="https://www.usa.gov/student-loans#item-206091">Student loans</a></li>
       <li><a href="https://www.usa.gov/buying-home">Housing loans</a>, including <a href="https://www.disasterassistance.gov/">disaster</a> and <a href="https://www.usa.gov/repairing-home#item-34757">home improvement loans</a></li>
       <li><a href="https://www.usa.gov/expand-business#item-37092">Small Business loans</a></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Search for Government Loans</h3>
      <p>Use the federal government’s free, official website, <a href="http://www.govloans.gov/">GovLoans.gov</a>, rather than commercial sites that may charge a fee for information or application forms.</p>
      <p>GovLoans.gov directs you to information on loans for agriculture, business, disaster relief, education, housing, and for veterans.</p>
      <ul>
       <li><strong>Eligibility -&nbsp;</strong>Use the <a href="https://www.govloans.gov/loans/loan-finder#benefits&amp;qc=cat_1">online pre-screening tool</a>.</li>
       <li><strong>How to apply</strong>&nbsp;- Each loan program has its own application process.</li>
      </ul>
