      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-214370">Military Benefits</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>Military members and veterans have access to valuable benefits. Some are for families too.</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p><strong>Health Care</strong> - The <a href="https://www.health.mil/">Military Health System</a> provides care for members, retirees, and&nbsp;dependents.</p></li>
       <li><p><strong>Housing </strong>- As a member or veteran, you can apply for <a href="https://www.usa.gov/veteran-housing#item-36637">home loans and grants from the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)</a>.</p></li>
       <li><p><strong>Education </strong>- You may qualify for <a href="https://www.usa.gov/education-military-veterans">education benefits</a> through military and veterans programs. You might even be able to <a href="https://www.va.gov/education/transfer-post-9-11-gi-bill-benefits/">transfer your Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits</a> to your spouse or children.</p></li>
       <li><p><strong>Life Insurance</strong> - All members are enrolled in <a href="https://www.usa.gov/veteran-burial-benefits#item-36197">service members' life insurance</a>. Retirees can enroll in the Survivor Benefit Plan.</p></li>
       <li><p><strong>Job Training and Help</strong> - Service members leaving the military can <a href="https://www.usa.gov/veteran-employment#item-35582">get help finding a civilian job</a>.</p></li>
       <li><p><strong>Retirement </strong>- Members who retire with at least 20 years of service <a href="https://www.usa.gov/military-pay#item-36297">get a monthly annuity for life</a>.</p></li>
      </ul> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-36812"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-36812">Military Assistance Programs for Veterans, Service Members, and Families</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>If you're a service member, veteran, or spouse, you’re not alone. The military and federal agencies have resources to help you manage life. &nbsp;Use these tools to get help with finances, health care, jobs, moving, and other challenges.</p>
      <h3>Manage Your Benefits and Records with milConnect</h3>
      <p>If you're a military member or a veteran, you can&nbsp;<a href="https://milconnect.dmdc.osd.mil/milconnect/">manage many of your benefits and records</a> in one place. MilConnect, a self-service portal, lets you:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Get copies of your Official Military Personnel File</p></li>
      </ul>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Update your family members in the Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System (DEERS)</p></li>
      </ul>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Transfer your GI Bill education benefits to family members</p></li>
      </ul>
      <ul>
       <li><p>View your health care coverage</p></li>
      </ul>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Update your work and personal contact information</p></li>
      </ul>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Find all the latest information and resources to manage your benefits</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>National Resource Directory for Service Members and Veterans</h3>
      <p>The National Resource Directory connects the military and veterans with local support. Find <a href="https://nrd.gov/">military assistance resources</a> for:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Jobs</p></li>
       <li><p>Health</p></li>
       <li><p>Homelessness</p></li>
       <li><p>Housing</p></li>
       <li><p>Caregiver support</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Financial Assistance for Service Members and Veterans</h3>
      <p>The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) <a href="https://www.consumerfinance.gov/practitioner-resources/servicemembers/">helps military families and veterans with financial issues</a> including:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p>Filing complaints</p></li>
       <li><p>Avoiding scams targeting military and veterans</p></li>
       <li><p>Improving money management skills</p></li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Military OneSource</h3>
      <p><a href="http://www.militaryonesource.mil/">Military OneSource</a> offers help with all aspects of military life. <a href="https://www.militaryonesource.mil/confidential-help">Counseling for service members and families</a> is free and confidential.</p>
      <p>Find information and resources in areas such as:</p>
      <ul>
       <li><p><a href="http://www.militaryonesource.mil/financial-legal">Military financial and legal assistance</a></p></li>
       <li><p><a href="https://www.militaryonesource.mil/education-employment/for-spouses/for-spouses-benefits">Job and education help for military spouses</a></p></li>
       <li><p><a href="http://www.militaryonesource.mil/parenting">Parenting</a> and <a href="https://www.militaryonesource.mil/-/child-care-the-essentials">military child care assistance</a></p></li>
       <li><p><a href="https://www.militaryonesource.mil/military-life-cycle/deployment/preparing-for-deployment">Deployment help for military families</a></p></li>
       <li><p><a href="https://www.militaryonesource.mil/moving-housing/moving">Moving assistance for military families</a></p></li>
       <li><p><a href="http://www.militaryonesource.mil/health-wellness">Military health and wellness, including:</a></p>
        <ul>
         <li><p>Preventing training injuries</p></li>
         <li><p>Dealing with family violence</p></li>
         <li><p>Managing stress and suicidal thoughts</p></li>
        </ul></li>
      </ul>
      <p>You can also call Military OneSource at 1-800-342-9647 24 hours a day, seven days a week. <a href="http://www.militaryonesource.mil/web/mos/contact-us">Visit their contact page</a> to learn how to call from outside the U.S. or from your computer.</p> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-214340"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-214340">Military Tax Benefits and Tax Preparation Help</h2> 
      </header> 
      <h3>Tax Benefits &nbsp;</h3>
      <p>The Internal Revenue&nbsp;Service (IRS) offers <a href="https://www.irs.gov/individuals/military">a list of tax benefits</a> for military, retirees, and veterans. Benefits include a combat pay exemption, tax breaks for military dependents, and other deductions.&nbsp;</p>
      <h3>Tax Preparation Help&nbsp;</h3>
      <ul>
       <li>Military OneSource offers service members MilTax, <a href="https://www.militaryonesource.mil/financial-legal/tax-resource-center/miltax-military-tax-services">a free tax preparation and filing software program</a>. They also provide free personalized help from tax consultants.</li>
       <li>The IRS offers an <a href="https://www.usa.gov/help-with-taxes#item-37047">interactive tax assistant and other tax tools </a>to help with general tax preparation.</li>
       <li><a href="https://militarypay.defense.gov/Portals/3/Documents/Blended%20Retirement/FINRED-NewTaxLaws-Booklet-10Dec2018(Final).pdf?ver=2019-01-30-171230-243">Learn more about the tax law changes</a> that may affect service members and their families. Changes include an increased child tax credit and new rules for deducting moving expenses.&nbsp;</li>
      </ul> 
     </article> 
     <article role="complementary" aria-labelledby="item-214221"> 
      <header> 
       <h2 id="item-214221">State and Federal Benefits for Military Families</h2> 
      </header> 
      <p>If you receive military or veterans benefits, you may also qualify for other state and federal benefits. This includes Social Security, unemployment benefits, health insurance coverage, welfare, and food assistance.</p>
      <ul>
       <li>Welfare and food assistance - Military and veteran families with a low income can get help with food costs from a <a href="https://www.usa.gov/food-help">wide range of nutrition programs</a>. This includes Supplemental Assistance Nutrition Program (SNAP) benefits, or “food stamps.” <a href="https://www.fns.usda.gov/get-involved/military-and-veteran-families">Learn how to apply for SNAP benefits</a>. You may also qualify for <a href="/benefits#item-36602">welfare or temporary assistance for needy families (TANF)</a>. This can give you help with housing, home energy, child care, job training, and more.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
       <li>Health benefits - Members of the military and their families receive a wide range of health coverage through <a href="https://www.tricare.mil">TRICARE health plans</a>. If you have a low income, you may also qualify for <a href="/benefits#item-35462">Medicaid or the Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP)</a>. Once you turn 65 or older, or meet other requirements, you can sign up for Medicare. <a href="https://www.tricare.mil/medicare">Get information on how to use Medicare and TRICARE together</a>.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
       <li>Social Security - <a href="https://www.ssa.gov/pubs/EN-05-10017.pdf">You can qualify for both Social Security benefits and military retirement</a>. You’ll receive your full Social Security benefits based on your earnings. To get your benefits, you must earn credits by working and paying Social Security taxes. <a href="/about-social-security#item-212313">Learn more about Social Security and how it works</a>.</li>
       <li>Unemployment benefits - If you recently left the military, you may be eligible for <a href="https://workforcesecurity.doleta.gov/unemploy/ucx.asp">unemployment compensation for ex-service members (UCX)</a>. You may also qualify for your state’s unemployment insurance program. These programs pay money to workers who become unemployed through no fault of their own. <a href="/unemployment#item-35882">Find out how to apply for unemployment benefits</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
     </article>
